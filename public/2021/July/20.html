Armed conflicts and attacks

  • Mali War
    • A man with a knife lunges at Malian President Assimi Goïta at a Bamako mosque, perhaps cutting his arm, and is immediately arrested. (Al Jazeera)
  • Israel–Lebanon relations
    • Israeli military shells target in southern Lebanon after rockets are fired from there into Israeli territory. Israeli Prime Minister Naftali Bennett says that "anyone who tries to harm us will pay a painful price in return". (AP)

Health and environment

  • COVID-19 pandemic
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Asia
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Iran
        • Iran reports a record 27,444 new cases of COVID-19 in the past 24 hours, thereby bringing the national total of cases to 3,576,148 cases. (AP)
        • Iran imposes a week-long lockdown in Tehran and its surrounding region in the province of Alborz where all bazaars, public offices, market place, restaurants, gyms, and movie theatres are closed, due to a rising number of the COVID-19 cases. (AP)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Laos
        • Laos reports a record 170 new cases of COVID-19 in the past 24 hours, thereby bringing the nationwide total of confirmed cases to 3,710. (The Laotian Times)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Myanmar
        • Nyan Win, a senior advisor to ousted leader Aung San Suu Kyi, dies in hospital after contracting COVID-19 in prison, at the age of 78, amid a surge of infections in the country. (Al Jazeera)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in South Korea
        • South Korean Prime Minister Kim Boo-kyum and defense minister apologize for the outbreak of COVID-19 aboard destroyer Munmu the Great off the coast of Africa. So far, almost 250 out of the 301 unvaccinated crew aboard the ship contracted COVID-19. Prime minister Kim says "I apologize for having failed to take better care of the health of our soldiers who devoted themselves to the country". (Reuters)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in the United States
      • COVID-19 pandemic in California
        • Pasadena becomes the first city in Southern California require vaccinations for employees. The mandate will not be in effect until a full approval by the FDA. (KCBS-TV)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Washington, D.C.
        • U.S. House Minority Whip Steve Scalise receives his first dose of the Pfizer COVID-19 vaccine at a clinic in the Jefferson Parish in Louisiana amid the spread of the Lineage B.1.617 Delta variant. (NOLA.com)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Australia
      • Lockdown restrictions return to South Australia after a rise in COVID-19 case numbers. Three Australian states are now under strict lockdowns. (The Guardian)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Russia
      • Russia surpasses six million cases of COVID-19. (AKIPress)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Zimbabwe, COVID-19 vaccination in Zimbabwe
      • Zimbabwean authorities orders a compulsory vaccinations for civil servants and reducing the number of government workers to physically report for duty to 25% a bid to stem a rise in local transmission of COVID-19. (RTÉ)

International relations

  • Lithuania–Taiwan relations
    • Taiwan announces it will be opening a representative office in Vilnius, Lithuania. This comes on the heels on warming relations between the two nations and the opening of a Lithuanian office in Taipei. The office will be known as the Taiwanese Representative Office in Lithuania, making it the first Taiwanese diplomatic mission to feature Taiwan's name. (Taiwan News)

Politics and elections

  • Belarusian opposition leader Sviatlana Tsikhanouskaya asks the Biden administration, during a visit to Washington, D.C., to impose sanctions on different companies in her native Belarus to put pressure to the government of President Alexander Lukashenko. (Reuters)

Science and technology

  • Blue Origin, Blue Origin NS-16
    • Blue Origin launches its first crew onboard its New Shepard rocket to outer space. The mission was crewed by American billionaire and Blue Origin founder Jeff Bezos, his brother Mark Bezos, pilot and Mercury 13 candidate Wally Funk and Dutch student Oliver Daemen. Funk and Daemen, age 82 and 18 respectively, become the oldest and youngest people ever to travel to space. (BBC News)
