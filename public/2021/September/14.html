Armed conflicts and attacks

  • Aftermath of the assassination of Jovenel Moïse
    • Prosecutor Bed-Ford Claude asks a judge to forbid prime minister Ariel Henry from leaving Haiti "by air, sea or road" due to "serious presumption relative to the assassination of the former president". (Reuters)

Health and environment

  • COVID-19 pandemic
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Asia
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Malaysia
        • Malaysia surpasses two million cases of COVID-19. (The Straits Times)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Singapore, COVID-19 vaccination in Singapore
        • Singapore begins a nationwide campaign for booster dose of the COVID-19 vaccine for people aged over 60 years and people with compromised immune system who have received their second dose at least six months ago amid the resurgence of COVID-19 infections. (Nikkei Asia)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Europe
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Italy
        • Italy surpasses 130,000 COVID-19-related deaths. (Il Messaggero)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in the Netherlands
        • Caretaker Prime Minister Mark Rutte announces that 1.5 m (4 ft 11 in) social distancing requirement will be scrapped on September 25 and instead at the same time will implement a "Corona pass" to enter bars, restaurants, and large-scale events and festivals as the number of COVID-19 cases declined. (MedicalXpress)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Russia
        • President Vladimir Putin enters self-isolation after members of his inner circle tested positive for COVID-19. (AP)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Africa
      • COVID-19 pandemic in South Sudan
        • South Sudan received its first consignment of 152,950 doses of the single-dose Janssen COVID-19 vaccines. (APO Group via The Guardian Nigeria)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Zimbabwe
        • The Parliament of Zimbabwe bans anyone who unvaccinated against COVID-19 from attending church service in order to boost the vaccination rate. (AFP via Barron's)

International relations

  • LGBT rights in Hungary, LGBT rights in Poland
    • At the end of a European Union summit, representatives threaten to fine Poland on a daily basis and withhold part of the EU's budget from Hungary over their laws against the gay community and LGBT community in general. (Bloomberg)

Politics and elections

  • 2021 United States gubernatorial elections
    • 2021 California gubernatorial recall election
      • California voters head to the polls to determine whether to remove or keep incumbent Governor Gavin Newsom. This will be the first recall election in California since 2003. (AP)
