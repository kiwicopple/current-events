Armed conflicts and attacks

  • Afghanistan conflict
    • Panjshir conflict
      • The Taliban says it now controls the Panjshir Valley, thus all of Afghanistan. Resistance leader Amrullah Saleh denies fleeing the country and says the resistance, also led by Ahmad Massoud, continues. (Reuters)
  • 2021 Auckland Countdown stabbing
    • Six people are injured, three critically, in a mass stabbing at a supermarket in LynnMall, Auckland, New Zealand. The attacker, a man under surveillance since 2016 for following ISIL online, is shot dead by police. (BBC) (RNZ)
  • Aftermath of the Second Libyan Civil War
    • Rival armed groups clash in Tripoli, in what is described as the worst confrontation since the ceasefire reached in 2020 between the Government of National Accord (GNA) and the Libyan National Army (LNA) forces, led by warlord Khalifa Haftar. (Reuters)

Health and environment

  • COVID-19 pandemic
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Asia
      • COVID-19 pandemic in North Korea
        • North Korean leader Kim Jong-un demand officials to wage a tougher epidemic prevention campaign in “our style” after he rejects offering of three million doses the COVID-19 vaccine via the U.N. COVAX initiative. (NBC News)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in the Philippines
        • COVID-19 vaccination in the Philippines
          • The Philippine Food and Drug Administration approves the emergency use of the Moderna COVID-19 vaccine for children ages 12 to 17. (Rappler)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Singapore
        • COVID-19 vaccination in Singapore
          • The Singaporean Health ministry will give a booster dose of the COVID-19 vaccine for people aged over 60 years old, people with immunocompromised and residents of nursing home facilities. The booster vaccine rollout expected to begin this month. (CNBC)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Vietnam
        • Vietnam reports a record 14,922 new cases of COVID-19 in the past 24 hours, surpassing 500,000 total. (The Star)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Oceania
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Australia
        • COVID-19 pandemic in New South Wales
          • New South Wales reports a record 1,431 new cases and 12 deaths from COVID-19 in the past 24 hours. (The Sydney Morning Herald)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in French Polynesia
        • French Polynesia extends the COVID-19 lockdown to September 20 following the deaths of more than 200 people over the past two weeks. The lockdown extension will apply to the Society Islands and the Tuamotus archipelago as cases rise in those regions. (RNZ)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in the United Kingdom, COVID-19 vaccination in the United Kingdom
      • The Joint Committee on Vaccination and Immunisation recommends that healthy children aged 12 to 15 should not be give a COVID-19 vaccine due to fact that children were a a low risk from the COVID-19 and that jabs would offer only a marginal benefit. (BBC)
  • Bangladesh reports a total of 10,981 dengue fever cases and 48 related deaths since the beginning of the year. (Al Jazeera)

International relations

  • Foreign relations of the European Union
    • The European Union, in a similar move taken by the United Kingdom, says that it will not recognize for now a Taliban government in Afghanistan and that it will be "subjected to conditions". It also states, however, that it will nevertheless engage the group in diplomatic talks. (Reuters)
  • Foreign relations of Chile
    • China and Chile jointly announce the opening of a Sinovac COVID-19 vaccine production plant in the Santiago Metropolitan Area. Health minister Enrique Paris says "it is a happy day for Chile and Latin American countries" because Chile would be able to export vaccines. (Al Jazeera)

Law and crime

  • Aftermath of the 2021 United States Capitol attack
    • Criminal charges brought in the 2021 United States Capitol attack
      • Jake Angeli, also known as Jacob Chansley and "the QAnon shaman", pleads guilty to obstruction of the electoral college vote count during the January 6 riot at the United States Capitol. His sentencing is scheduled for November 17. (CBS News)
  • Catholic Church sexual abuse cases
    • Defrocked Catholic Cardinal Theodore McCarrick pleads not guilty to three counts of indecent assault and battery on a 16-year-old boy at a 1974 wedding reception in Massachusetts. The statute of limitations paused when he left the state shortly after the alleged incident. He is the first current or former American cardinal to be charged with a sex crime. (DW)
  • The president of Vanuatu, Obed Moses Tallis, pardons former prime ministers Charlot Salwai, Joe Natuman and Serge Vohor, all convicted of political and financial crimes, including bribery and corruption. (RNZ)

Politics and elections

  • 2021 Liberal Democratic Party leadership election
    • Yoshihide Suga, the Prime Minister of Japan, announces he will not seek re-election after one year in office. He had replaced former Prime Minster Shinzo Abe who resigned for health reasons in September 2020. (CNN)
