Arts and culture

  • Historical Memory Law
    • The last statue of Spanish dictator Francisco Franco in Spain is removed in Melilla. (El País)

Health and environment

  • COVID-19 pandemic
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Europe
      • COVID-19 pandemic in the United Kingdom
        • COVID-19 pandemic in Northern Ireland
          • Northern Ireland reports their first three cases of 501.V2 variant first detected in South Africa. (The Irish Times)
        • COVID-19 pandemic in Scotland
          • First Minister Nicola Sturgeon announces the roadmap to ease lockdown, with four people from two households will be allowed to meet outdoors from March 15 and a stay-at-home order will be lifted on April 5. While non-essential sectors such as bars, restaurants, gymns, and leisure activities can be reopen on April 26, Scotland will return to levels system at the same time. (BBC)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in the Czech Republic
        • Former president Václav Klaus tested positive for COVID-19. (Aktuálně.cz)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in the Netherlands
        • Prime Minister Mark Rutte extends the curfew until March 15 while eases some lockdown restrictions, with senior high schools, vocational schools, hairdressers, and masseurs can reopen in March. (ABC News)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in the Republic of Ireland
        • Taoiseach Micheál Martin announces the phased reopening of schools from March 1 starting with junior and senior infants as well as senior students facing Leaving Certificate exams. Meanwhile, the government extends the level 5 lockdown restrictions until April 5. (The Guardian)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Ukraine
        • Ukraine receives the first batch, consisting of 500 doses, of Oxford–AstraZeneca's vaccine manufactured by the Serum Institute of India. (RFE/RL)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Asia
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Afghanistan
        • Afghanistan begins a vaccination campaign against COVID-19 following the WHO's emergency use approval of Oxford–AstraZeneca's vaccine. (Anadolu Agency)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Bangladesh
        • Bangladesh receives the second batch, consisting two million doses, of Oxford–AstraZeneca's vaccine from the Serum Institute of India. (All India Radio)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Oman, Travel restrictions related to the COVID-19 pandemic
        • Oman announces the suspension of passengers' entry from 10 countries for 15 days beginning from February 25 with exception of Omani citizens, diplomats, health workers and their families. (Gulf Business)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Africa
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Egypt
        • Egypt receives 300,000 doses of China's Sinopharm vaccine. It is the third vaccine to be delivered to country and the second to be delivered by Sinopharm. (Asharq Al-awsat)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Senegal
        • Senegal begins a vaccination campaign against COVID-19 using 200,000 doses of China's Sinopharm vaccine. Government ministers and health workers are the first to receive the shots. (U.S. News and World Report)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in the United States
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Washington (state)
        • Washington reports their first case of 501.V2 variant first detected in South Africa in King County. (The Seattle Times)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Suriname
      • Suriname begins a vaccination campaign against COVID-19 using small batch of vaccine doses donated from Barbados. (Reuters)
  • Guinea kicks off a vaccination campaign against Ebola for high-risk people in Gouecke, Nzérékoré Prefecture, as an emergency measures to curb the spread of the virus that was detected for the first time since 2016. (WHO Regional Office for Africa)

International relations

  • Joint Comprehensive Plan of Action
    • Iran says it is restricting the access of international inspectors to its nuclear installations. (AP via Al Arabiya)

Law and crime

  • Crime in Italy
    • A man stabs and wounds two police officers and tries to stab two passersbys, who escape unharmed, during a random stabbing rampage in Milan, Lombardy. The attacker is shot dead by police. (Milano Today)
  • Malaysia deports 1,086 Burmese citizens back to Myanmar, defying an order by the Kuala Lumpur High Court halting their deportation in light of the February 1 coup. (Reuters)

Science and technology

  • News Media Bargaining Code
    • Facebook unblocks Australian news pages after the Australian government offers to make it exempt from their new News Media Bargaining Code if Facebook, Inc. can form enough private agreements to pay Australian news companies for content. (The Guardian)

Sports

  • Golfer Tiger Woods is involved in a serious car accident in Rancho Palos Verdes, California where he sustains multiple leg injuries, requiring surgery. (CNN)
