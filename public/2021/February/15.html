Armed conflicts and attacks

  • Yemeni Civil War
    • Saudi Arabian–Yemeni border conflict
      • The Houthis say that they have struck Saudi Arabia's Abha International Airport and King Abdulaziz International Airport with drones. A Houthi spokesman said on Twitter that the attacks halted operations in the airports for two hours. The Saudi-led coalition says it intercepted drones towards the kingdom but does not confirm the attacks. (Reuters)
  • Syrian civil war
    • Iran–Israel conflict during the Syrian Civil War
      • Israeli airstrikes on Iranian weapons and missile depots near Damascus, Syria, kill six foreign nationals, according to the Syrian Observatory for Human Rights. The headquarters of the Syrian Army's 4th Armoured Division is also hit. (Jewish Press)
  • 2021 Myanmar protests, aftermath of the 2021 Myanmar coup d'état
    • Myanmar's military junta deploys Tatmadaw troops and cuts Internet around the country hours after security forces fired live rounds to disperse a demonstration in northern state of Kachin. (The Straits Times)
  • 2021 Erbil missile attacks
    • Multiple rockets strike near Erbil International Airport in Erbil, Iraq, killing a foreign civilian contractor and wounding five others, including a U.S. service member. (Reuters)

Business and economy

  • Economic impact of the COVID-19 pandemic, COVID-19 recession
    • Economy of Japan
      • Japan's GDP contracts 4.8 per cent in 2020, which is the first annual economic contraction since 2009 due to the impact of the pandemic. (BBC)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Denmark, Economy of Denmark
      • Denmark’s s GDP contracts 3.7 per cent in 2020, which is the first annual economic contraction since 2009 due to the impact of the pandemic. (DR)
  • The World Trade Organization formally appoints former Finance Minister of Nigeria Ngozi Okonjo-Iweala as its first African and female Director-General. (Bloomberg)

Disasters and accidents

  • A passenger barge crashes and overturns on the Congo River in Mai-Ndombe Province, Democratic Republic of the Congo, killing at least 60 people, while 240 others are reported missing. At least 300 people have been rescued from the river. (Reuters)

Health and environment

  • COVID-19 pandemic
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Oceania
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Australia
        • Travel restrictions related to the COVID-19 pandemic
          • Australia suspends its quarantine-free travel bubble arrangement with New Zealand after the discovery of three new community cases in Auckland. (BBC)
        • Australia receives 142,000 doses of Pfizer–BioNTech vaccine in Sydney. (ABC Australia)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in New Zealand
        • New Zealand receives around 60,000 doses of Pfizer–BioNTech vaccine in Auckland. (Stuff.co.nz)
        • Auckland enters level three restrictions for three days in response to an ongoing COVID-19 outbreak, shutting all non-essential businesses and in-person education. The rest of the country enters level 2 restrictions, which limit public gatherings. (The Guardian)
        • Prime Minister Jacinda Ardern confirms that three members of a family in Auckland who tested positive for COVID-19 are infected with UK variant and not connected to managed isolation and quarantine facilities. (TVNZ)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Asia
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Cambodia
        • Cambodia reports the first cases of B.1.1.7 variant first detected in the United Kingdom in two Indian people and a 23-year-old Chinese woman who arrived in the country. (Khmer Times)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Iraq
        • Iraq announces they will reimpose a partial lockdown from February 18 until March 8 after reports of the first cases of the UK variant of SARS-CoV-2. (Arab News)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Japan
        • Japan surpasses 7,000 deaths of COVID-19. (ABS-CBN News)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in South Korea
        • The Korea Disease Control and Prevention Agency scales back Q1 vaccination targets to 750,000 due to delayed shipments from COVAX and the decision not to use AstraZeneca's vaccine for people aged 65 or older until more efficacy data becomes avaliable. (Reuters)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Vietnam
        • Vietnamese authorities order two million people in Hải Dương province to stay at home for the next 15 days effective tomorrow due to a growing COVID-19 outbreak in the province. Gatherings with more than two people are banned, and when leaving their homes, residents must stay 2 metres (6.6 ft) from others. (CNA)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in the United Kingdom
      • COVID-19 pandemic in England
        • The United Kingdom begins a mandatory hotel quarantine for all British, Irish citizens and permanent residents arriving in England from 33 "high risk" countries, meaning they are required to stay for 10 days at government-designated accomodations. (ITV) (The Irish Times)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Scotland
        • Scotland begins a mandatory hotel quarantine for all international arrivals, which they are required to self-isolated for 10 days in six government-designated hotels. (STV)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in the United States
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Connecticut
        • Connecticut reports the first case of 501.V2 variant in a Fairfield County resident between the ages of 60-70 who had no travel history. (WVIT-TV)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Colombia
      • Colombia receives the first shipment consisting 50,000 doses of Pfizer-BioNTech vaccine. (MedicalXpress)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Zimbabwe
      • Zimbabwe receives the first shipment of 200,000 doses of Sinopharm's vaccine in Harare. (South China Morning Post)
    • COVID-19 vaccine
      • The World Health Organization announces the approval of the Oxford-AstraZeneca vaccine. (Forbes)
  • An Ebola vaccination campaign begins in the city of Butembo, in eastern Democratic Republic of the Congo, where health workers at Matanda health center are the first to be vaccinated. (Reuters)

International relations

  • Turkey–United States relations, Operation Claw-Eagle 2
    • Turkish President Recep Tayyip Erdoğan accuses the United States of supporting "the PKK terrorists", following the killing of 13 Turkish hostages held captive in Iraqi Kurdistan. Turkey also summons the U.S. ambassador David M. Satterfield in response to the killings. (Daily Sabah)
  • Nuclear program of Iran
    • Iran says it has informed the International Atomic Energy Agency that it will no longer allow snap inspections of its nuclear facilities from February 21. The snap inspections were a condition of the 2015 Iranian nuclear deal. (Reuters)

Law and crime

  • Aftermath of the 2021 storming of the United States Capitol
    • House Speaker Nancy Pelosi announces that Congress will establish a 9/11-styled commission to investigate the January 6 riot at the United States Capitol. (AP)
  • Turkish authorities announce the detention of 718 people it accuses of having links to Kurdish militant groups. Among those arrested include pro-Kurdish Peoples' Democratic Party (HDP) officials. The mass arrests are seen as a direct response to the murder of 13 Turkish hostages in Iraq. The PKK blamed Turkish airstrikes on their bases for the deaths of the hostages. (France 24)

Sports

  • 2021 NASCAR Cup Series
    • Michael McDowell wins the 63rd running of the Daytona 500. It is McDowell's first career victory in the Cup Series. (NASCAR.com)
