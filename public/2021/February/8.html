Armed conflicts and attacks

  • Yemeni Civil War
    • Ma'rib Campaign
      • Houthi forces resume an offensive against Maʼrib, the last stronghold of the internationally-recognized Yemeni government in northern Yemen. A government source says 20 pro-government fighters have been killed and 28 others injured after repelling "five attacks" in the past 24 hours. (Al Arabiya)

Disasters and accidents

  • Twenty-four workers die in a flooded illegally-run textile workshop in a private house in Tangier, Morocco, which occurred as a result of intense rains that hit the region. Ten others are rescued and hospitalized. (Al Jazeera)
  • Six people are killed after a semi-trailer truck collided with a car in Anyuan County, east China's Jiangxi province.[1]

Health and environment

  • COVID-19 pandemic
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Asia
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Indonesia
        • Indonesia reports a record 13,038 recoveries in the past 24 hours, bringing the nationwide total of recoveries to 963,028. (detikHealth)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Laos
        • Laos receives 300,000 doses of China's Sinopharm vaccine. (Bangkok Post)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Malaysia
        • Malaysia reports a record 24 deaths in the past 24 hours, bringing the nationwide death toll to 896. (Malay Mail)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Europe
      • COVID-19 pandemic in France, Impact of the COVID-19 pandemic on education
        • France introduces new measures to ban homemade masks in educational institutions. It requires school children over the age of 6 to wear category 1 masks in schools. (RFI)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in the Netherlands
        • The Netherlands extends their nationwide curfew, which was introduced for the first time since the Second World War, until March 3 in order to prevent the spread of B.1.1.7 variant first detected in the United Kingdom. (AP)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in the United States
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Texas
        • The first case of 501.V2 variant was confirmed in Houston in a man from Fort Bend County who tested positive and recovered weeks ago, making Texas the fourth state to discover a South African variant. (Houston-Chronicle)
      • The United States surpasses 27 million cases of COVID-19. (New York Daily News)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Argentina
      • Argentina detects their first cases of two different Brazilian SARS-CoV-2 variants in two travellers infected with Amazonas P.1 variant and two others infected with Rio de Janeiro P.2 variant. (Semana)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Mexico
      • Mexican President Andrés Manuel López Obrador resumes morning news conferences two-weeks after testing positive from COVID-19. (AP)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Rwanda
      • Rwanda eases their lockdown in the capital Kigali and allows businesses to resume with not more than 30% essential staff as the transmission rate has slowed. (Anadolu Agency)

Law and crime

  • Trial of Benjamin Netanyahu, Corruption in Israel
    • Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu pleads not guilty to charges of bribery, breach of trust and fraud at the Jerusalem District Court. (Reuters)
  • Venezuelan refugee crisis
    • The Government of Colombia announces the legalization of undocumented Venezuelan migrants currently in the country, allowing them eligible to receive 10-year residency permits. (Associated Press)


Politics and elections

  • 2021 Myanmar protests, aftermath of the 2021 Myanmar coup d'état
    • A nationwide general strike takes place in Myanmar in response to the military coup with thousands marching in the capital Naypyidaw, where they clashed with riot police. Several injuries are reported. (BBC)
    • The military junta declares martial law in the country's second largest city Mandalay amid protests against the coup. Gatherings of more than five people in public are banned, and a curfew will run from 8pm until 4am. (Channel News Asia)
  • 2021 Somali presidential election
    • An alliance of opposition parties cease recognising Mohamed Abdullahi Mohamed as President, and propose the creation of a "national council of lawmakers, opposition leaders and civil society" to govern the country as the President's term expires with no clear plan for succession. (Reuters)
  • The Palau National Congress passes a joint resolution supporting the decision made by President Surangel Whipps Jr. to leave the Pacific Islands Forum. The National Congress says the "gentleman's agreement was not honored". (RNZ)

Sports

  • 2021 Australian Open, Impact of the COVID-19 pandemic on sports
    • The Grand Slam of Australian Open begins in Melbourne Park, three weeks later than originally scheduled due to the coronavirus pandemic, with daily attendance capped at 30,000 a day. (7 News)
