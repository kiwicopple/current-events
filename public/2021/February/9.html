Armed conflicts and attacks

  • War in Afghanistan
    • 2021 Afghanistan attacks
      • Five people are killed in two separate attacks targeting government employees in Kabul, including the head of the Ministry of Rural Rehabilitation and Development. (Reuters)

Health and environment

  • COVID-19 pandemic
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Europe
      • COVID-19 pandemic in the United Kingdom
        • COVID-19 pandemic in England
          • Health Secretary Matt Hancock announces that all British and Irish residents returning from 33 "high-risk" countries will be charged £1,750 to stay in quarantine hotels in England for 10 days and those who fail to do so or violating COVID rules face fines of up to £10,000 or up to 10 years in jail. (BBC)
        • COVID-19 pandemic in Scotland
          • Scottish Transport secretary Michael Matheson introduces a tougher rules that all people arrived from outside the UK and Ireland will go immediately into “managed quarantine” at one of six hotels at Aberdeen, Edinburgh and Glasgow airports, which cost £1,750. (The Independent)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Austria
        • Chancellor Sebastian Kurz announces a statewide isolation order in Tyrol beginning from February 12 to prevent the spread of 501.V2 variant, which Schwaz District becomes the European epicentre of this variant. People will have to present negative COVID-19 testing result over the last 48 hours to leave the state. (ABC News)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Belarus
        • Belarus surpasses 260,000 cases of COVID-19 after reporting 561 new cases. (Xinhua)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Greece
        • Prime Minister Kyriakos Mitsotakis announce a near-full lockdown in Attica region, which includes the capital Athens, starting from February 11 until February 28. All schools, churches, hair salons, and retail stores, with the exception of supermarkets and pharmacies, are ordered to close; and restaurants are only allowed to offer delivery services. (Ekathimerini)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Spain
        • Spain surpasses three million cases of COVID-19. (MedicalXpress)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Switzerland
        • Switzerland’s Federal Office of Public Health reports its first case of Brazilian P.7 variant of SARS-CoV-2. (The Local Switzerland)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Asia
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Iran
        • Iran begins a vaccination campaign against COVID-19 among healthcare workers using the Sputnik V vaccine. (Al Jazeera)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Mongolia
        • Mongolia approves the emergency use of Russia's Sputnik V vaccine without additional clinical trials in the country. (Sputnik V)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Pakistan
        • Pakistan approves the emergency use of Russia's Sputnik V vaccine, becoming the third vaccine to be approved in the country after the Sinopharm and Oxford-AstraZeneca vaccines. (Reuters)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Canada
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Manitoba
        • Manitoba reports its first case of UK variant of SARS-CoV-2. (CBC)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Equatorial Guinea
      • Equatorial Guinea imposes a curfew between 7:00 p.m. to 6:00 a.m. local time for the first since the beginning of the pandemic and closes many leisure activities. It also reduces domestic flights to once per day, and international flights to twice per week for domestic carriers and one per week for international carriers. (EWN)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Peru
      • Peru launches a vaccination program against COVID-19 two days after it received 300,000 doses of BBIBP-CorV from Sinopharm. President Francisco Sagasti becomes the first person to get vaccinated. (Voice of America)

International relations

  • Five countries — Palau, Federated States of Micronesia, the Marshall Islands, Kiribati and Nauru — officially withdraw from the Pacific Islands Forum over disagreement regarding the choice of the new Secretary-General. (The Guardian)

Law and crime

  • Second impeachment trial of Donald Trump
    • Former United States president Donald Trump's second impeachment trial begins. (Reuters)
    • The United States Senate votes 56-44 to proceed with the impeachment trial, rejecting Trump attorney claims that the proceeding is unconstitutional after viewing January 6 Capitol riot. (RTÉ)
  • Mass media in Hungary
    • A court in Budapest rejects an appeal by independent radio station Klubrádió to have its broadcast license renewed by the government's media regulator, which says that the station had infringed rules. Klubrádió owner András Arató says the station will appeal at the Supreme Court. (AFP via Barron's)

Politics and elections

  • COVID-19 pandemic in Ghana, Impact of the COVID-19 pandemic on politics
    • The Parliament of Ghana suspends their sitting for three weeks after 17 MPs and 151 staff tested positive for COVID-19. (Pulse Ghana)
  • 2021 Myanmar protests, aftermath of the 2021 Myanmar coup d'état
    • Demonstrations in Naypyidaw, the capital of Myanmar, continue in response to the military coup. Riot police fire rubber bullets and use water cannons to disperse protesters. (BBC)
