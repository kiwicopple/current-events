Armed conflicts and attacks

  • 2021 Myanmar protests
    • Two people are killed and 40 others injured in a clash between police and demonstrators in Mandalay as the police fire live ammunition to suppress protesters and force workers back to their jobs. (The New York Times)
  • Syrian civil war
    • At least 130 Russian airstrikes have killed 21 members of Islamic State of Iraq and the Levant over the last 24 hours in the Badia desert, according to the opposition Syrian Observatory for Human Rights. (France 24)

Disasters and accidents

  • 2020–21 North American winter
    • U.S. President Joe Biden declares a major disaster in Texas following a severe winter storm and cold weather that left at least 70 people dead and millions others without power. It allows federal funds to be spent and make it available to affected individuals, including assistance for temporary housing and home repairs and low-cost loans. (NBC News)

Health and environment

  • COVID-19 pandemic
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Europe
      • COVID-19 pandemic in the United Kingdom
        • COVID-19 pandemic in England
          • The British Health Secretary announces a small step to ease the nation's lockdown, allowing care home visitors in England to be regularly visited by a single friend or family member. This change will takes effect on March 8. (BBC)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Hungary
        • Hungary surpasses 400,000 cases of COVID-19. (Hungary Today)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in the Republic of Ireland
        • Irish health officials say that the three cases of the Brazilian variant of SARS-CoV-2 have been contained. (Reuters)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Russia
        • Prime Minister Mikhail Mishustin announces the approval of CoviVac vaccine for domestic use in Russia, becoming the third vaccine to be approved despite clinical trials have yet to begin. (CNBC)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Asia
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Israel
        • Health Ministry said in a data that COVID-19 vaccines are 99.2% effective against serious illness, reduces mortality by 95.8% and decreases the chance of hospitalization by 98.9%. (The Times of Israel)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Japan
        • Japan reports a case of vaccine recipient breaking out in hives at a hospital in Toyama Prefecture following COVID-19 vaccination. (The Japan Times)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in the Philippines
        • Philippines reports 239 new deaths in the past 24 hours, which is the second-highest number of daily reported deaths since the beginning of the pandemic, bringing the nationwide death toll to 12,068. (CNA)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Taiwan
        • Taiwan grants emergency use authorization for the Oxford–AstraZeneca vaccine. (The Straits Times)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in New Zealand
      • New Zealand begins a vaccination programme against COVID-19, starting with 70 border workers under strict infection prevention measures. (RNZ)
  • 2020–21 H5N8 outbreak
    • Russia alerts the World Health Organization that it reports the first case of human transmission of highly pathogenic H5N8 bird flu in seven workers of a poultry farm in southern part of the country. (The Daily Telegraph)

Law and crime

  • The Moscow City Court upholds the three-year prison sentence against opposition politician and anti-corruption activist Alexei Navalny. Navalny now faces slander charges. His defense had argued before the court that the European Court of Human Rights had labeled his arrest as "unlawful". (DW)
  • Looting, barricades, and riots are reported during the fifth consecutive night of protests mainly in Barcelona, Spain, over the imprisonment of rapper Pablo Hasél. Several protesters break windows of the UNESCO World Heritage Site of Palau de la Música Catalana and try to storm the Stock Exchange of Barcelona. (ABC.es in Spanish)

Politics and elections

  • 2020-2021 Thai protests, COVID-19 pandemic in Thailand, Impact of the COVID-19 pandemic on politics
    • Prime Minister Prayut Chan-o-cha survives a second vote of no-confidence in the House of Representatives as he faces accusations over mismanaged economy, handling of the COVID-19 pandemic, corruption, and abuse of human rights. (The Washington Post)
