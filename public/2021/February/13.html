Armed conflicts and attacks

  • War in Afghanistan
    • 2021 Afghanistan attacks
      • At least four security forces personnel, including a commander, are killed in eastern and southern Afghanistan as clashes with an unidentified terrorist group intensify. Separately, in Kandahar, a Humvee packed with explosives detonates, injuring seven police officers. (Reuters)

Disasters and accidents

  • 2021 Fukushima earthquake
    • A 7.1 magnitude earthquake shakes the Tōhoku coast and nearby regions in Japan injuring at least 50 people. (The Japan Times)
  • A series of bad weather-related incidents in Northern Italy, leaves 5 people dead and 25 others wounded. (Il Messaggero)

Health and environment

  • COVID-19 pandemic
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Asia
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Lebanon
        • Lebanon receives the first shipment containing 28,500 doses of Pfizer-BioNTech vaccine from Belgium. (Arab News)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Pakistan
        • Two 11-week-old white tiger cubs that died in Lahore Zoo on January 30 appear to have died of COVID-19. (Hindustan Times)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in South Korea
        • The government decides to lower social distancing levels to level 2 in Seoul Capital Area and level 1.5 in other regions from February 15, allowing nightclub facilities to reopen and extending the operating hours of restaurants, coffee shops and gyms in Seoul Capital Area until 10 p.m. local time. However, gatherings of more than five people remain prohibited but theatres, libraries, and other types of businesses will be no longer subject to operating hours restrictions. (Bloomberg)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in the United Arab Emirates
        • The United Arab Emirates surpasses 1,000 total deaths from COVID-19. (Khaleej Times)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Australia
      • Victoria returns to Stage 4 restrictions in a five-day "circuit breaker" lockdown due to an ongoing outbreak of the B.1.1.7 variant in Holiday Inn at Melbourne Airport. All non-essential businesses are closed, masks are made mandatory in all public places, and people are only permitted to leave their homes for essential work, education or care-giving, or for up to 2 hours a day of essential shopping and outdoor exercise within a 5 kilometres (3.1 mi) radius of their homes. All international flights into Victoria have been cancelled for the duration of the lockdown. (ABC Australia)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Northern Cyprus
      • Northern Cyprus extends their full-scale curfew to stem the spread of COVID-19, which exempt pharmacies, groceries, supermarkets, bakeries and stores selling basic supplies, until February 22. (Anadolu Agency)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in South Africa
      • The cabinet decides to reopen 20 South Africa's land borders with Botswana, Lesotho, Eswatini, Namibia, Mozambique and Zimbabwe from February 15. (TimesLIVE)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Venezuela
      • Venezuela receives the first shipment of 100,000 doses of Sputnik V vaccine. (Reuters)

Law and crime

  • Second impeachment trial of Donald Trump
    • The United States Senate votes to acquit former president Donald Trump for the second time when the 57–43 vote in favor of conviction fails to reach the necessary two-thirds majority. Seven Republicans join the entire Democratic caucus in voting for conviction. (The Hill)

Politics and elections

  • 2021 Italian government crisis, Draghi Cabinet
    • Prime Minister Mario Draghi and the ministers of his cabinet officially are sworn in at the Quirinal Palace, ending the government crisis. (ANSA)

Sports

  • 2021 Australian Open, Impact of the COVID-19 pandemic on sports
    • Spectators at a late-running tennis match are ejected as Victoria's 5-day "circuit breaker" lockdown comes into effect. Matches at the Melbourne tournament will continue without crowds for the duration of the lockdown. (9 News)
