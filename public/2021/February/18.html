Business and economy

  • News Media Bargaining Code
    • Facebook bans Australian news publishers from posting and Australian users from viewing or sharing any news content in a preemptive retaliation against the Australian Competition & Consumer Commission's proposed News Media Bargaining Code, which would require it to pay to host Australian news content. A number of key government and charity organisations were also blocked for several hours. (The Guardian)
    • Alphabet Inc. agrees to begin paying News Corp for news content. News Corp CEO Robert Thomson credits the ACCC's proposed legislation with making the deal possible. (The New York Times)

Health and environment

  • COVID-19 pandemic
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Oceania
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Australia
        • Lockdown restrictions are eased in Victoria as contact tracers are confident all cases linked to a recent outbreak are self-isolating. People are permitted to leave their homes for any reason, but required to wear a mask indoors. Private gatherings of up to 5 people and public gatherings of up to 20 are permitted once again. Non-essential businesses and on-campus learning are reopened, and businesses that were working from home can return up to 50% of their workforce to the office. (The Guardian)
        • South Australia reopens its land border with Victoria to the state's regional population, while still restricting travellers from Melbourne, the location of the recent outbreak. Similar restrictions remain at the New South Wales and Tasmania borders. (9 News)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in New Zealand
        • Restrictions ease in New Zealand, with Auckland downgrading to alert level 2 and the rest of the country to alert level 1. Most Auckland schools are reopened and masks are made mandatory on public transport. (New Zealand Herald)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Asia
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Hong Kong
        • Hong Kong grants emergency use authorization to Sinovac's CoronaVac vaccine, making it the city's second vaccine to be approved. (Quartz)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Malaysia
        • Malaysia reports a record 25 new deaths in the past 24 hours and surpasses 1,000 deaths of COVID-19. (The Star)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Nepal
        • Nepal approves the emergency use of Sinopharm's vaccine, which become the second vaccine to be approved in the country. (Deccan Herald)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Saudi Arabia
        • Saudi Arabia's Food and Drug Authority approves the use of Oxford–AstraZeneca vaccine. (Gulf News)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Africa
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Nigeria
        • Nigeria approves the emergency use of Oxford–AstraZeneca vaccine produced by the Serum Institute of India. (Bloomberg)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Zimbabwe
        • Zimbabwe begins its vaccination programme against COVID-19 starting with health workers using China's Sinopharm vaccine. Health minister and vice president Constantino Chiwenga is the first person to receive the vaccine. (Anadolu Agency)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in the United States
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Nevada
        • Nevada reports their first case of 501.V2 variant in a person who travelled from South Africa and began showing symptoms of COVID-19 when arrived in Reno. (U.S. News and World Report)
    • COVID-19 vaccine, Variants of SARS-CoV-2
      • Lab studies suggest the Pfizer–BioNTech and Moderna COVID-19 vaccines are significantly less effective in providing antibody protection against the 501.V2 variant of the disease, most prevalent in South Africa. (The Guardian)

Science and technology

  • Mars 2020
    • The rover Perserverance successfully lands on Mars as part of the Mars 2020 mission. (The New York Times)
