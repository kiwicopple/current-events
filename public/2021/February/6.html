Armed conflicts and attacks

  • Internal conflict in Myanmar
    • An ambush on a convoy by suspected MNDAA insurgents in Shan State, Myanmar, kills nine civilians and three policemen, and injures 13 others. (ANI News)
  • Tigray War
    • The United Nations says the Ethiopian government is blocking aid from going to non-government-controlled rural areas, in the process blocking off aid from going to 2.3 million people who are in dire need of assistance. The rural areas of Tigray account for two-thirds of the Tigray population. Aid is only available for people living in towns along the main roads from Mekelle to Shire. (VOA)

Disasters and accidents

  • Eleven villagers are dead and three others rescued after their wooden boat overturns in a lake in Susong County, in east China's Anhui province. (Sina)

Health and environment

  • COVID-19 pandemic
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Asia
      • COVID-19 pandemic in mainland China
        • China's National Medical Products Administration gives a conditional approval of Sinovac's Coronavac vaccine for general use. (France 24)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Mongolia
        • Mongolia reports a record 61 new cases in the past 24 hours, bringing the nationwide total of confirmed cases to 1,989. (Xinhua)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Myanmar
        • Myanmar approves the use of Sputnik V vaccine developed by Russia. (Sputnik V)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in South Korea
        • The government eases the restrictions of businesses operating hours outside Seoul Capital Area, allowing those businesses to operate until 10 pm local time amid a backlash over prolonged virus curbs. (The Korea Herald)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Europe
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Italy
        • Italy signs a decree authorizing the emergency use of monoclonal antibodies to treat COVID-19 patients. (Reuters)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in the Netherlands
        • The Netherlands surpasses one million cases of COVID-19. (NL Times)

International relations

  • The leaders of Palau, Federated States of Micronesia, Nauru, Kiribati and the Marshall Islands are set to meet on Monday to discuss leaving the Pacific Islands Forum after Marshallese candidate Gerald Zackios was not chosen as Secretary-General. Palau had earlier announced that it was leaving the forum. The meeting was called by Nauruan President Lionel Aingimea. (RNZ)

Law and crime

  • 2021 Myanmar coup d'état
    • An Australian economic advisor to Aung San Suu Kyi reveals that he had been detained by the Tatmadaw. He is the first foreign national confirmed to be in the custody of Myanmar's military. (AFP via The Star)
  • After a juggler is shot dead by a Carabinero, several protests are held in Panguipulli, southern Chile. The protests escalate into riots and several buildings, including the city hall, are burned. (The New York Times)
  • Egypt frees Al Jazeera journalist Mahmoud Hussein after four years in detention. He had been held on charges of spreading false news, joining a banned group and receiving foreign funds. (VOA)

Science and technology

  • Censorship in Myanmar, aftermath of the 2021 Myanmar coup d'état
    • The military junta shuts down the country's internet with a near-total blackout in effect amid growing protests against the coup. (BBC)
