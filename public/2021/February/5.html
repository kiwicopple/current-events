Armed conflicts and attacks

  • Israeli–Palestinian conflict
    • The International Criminal Court announces that they will pave the way for investigations into alleged Israeli war crimes as well as Hamas in Palestinian territories. (NBC News)

Business and economy

  • Economic impact of the COVID-19 pandemic in the United States, American Rescue Plan
    • The United States Senate votes 51–50 to pass a budget resolution as a step to pass the $1.9 trillion stimulus package without Republican support, with Kamala Harris casting her first tiebreaking vote as Vice President. (CBS News)
    • The United States House of Representatives votes 219–209 to approve the Senate's budget resolution, with all Republicans and 1 Democrat voting against the resolution. (The Hill)

Health and environment

  • COVID-19 pandemic
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Europe
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Spain
        • COVID-19 pandemic in the Community of Madrid
          • Madrid reports Spain's first case of Lineage P.1 variant in a 44-year-old man travelled from Brazil on January 29 and tested positive for COVID-19 on antibody test at Adolfo Suárez Madrid–Barajas Airport. (RTVE)
        • Spain approves the use of Oxford-AstraZeneca vaccine in people aged 18 to 55. (El Mundo)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Greece
        • Greece imposes a 10-day curfew in Attica, Thessaloniki and Halkidiki, and close high schools in these areas beginning tomorrow in a bid to halt the rise of COVID-19 cases. (Ekathimerini)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Poland
        • Prime Minister Mateusz Morawiecki announces that hotels, theatres, swimming pools and other indoor facilities will be allowed to operated with 50 percent capacity from February 12, but restaurants and fitness centres will remain closed. (The National Herald)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Ukraine
        • Ukraine secures 12 million doses of the vaccine developed by AstraZeneca and Novavax. (UNIAN)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Asia
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Bahrain
        • Bahrain orders indoor gyms, indoor excercise classes, sports halls and swimming pools to close from February 7 until February 21 in order to curb the spread of COVID-19. It also allows government entities and institutions to have 70 percent of employees to work from home. (Arab News)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Israel
        • Israel announces that it would ease their lockdown measures beginning February 7 when people will no longer be restricted to within one kilometer of their homes, service will be allowed to operated, and national parks reopened. International borders will remain closed until February 20. (MedicalXpress)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in South Korea
        • South Korea surpasses 80,000 cases of COVID-19. (The Korea Herald)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Oceania
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Australia
        • Western Australia lifts a five-day lockdown in Perth and surrounding areas after it had reported no community case. (9 News)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in New Zealand
        • Immigration and government officials announce that New Zealand will restart the Refugee Quota Programme this month, nearly a year after the border was closed due to the pandemic. (RNZ)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in the United States
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Virginia
        • Virginia reports their first case of 501.V2 variant in a resident in eastern Virginia. (Richmond Times-Dispatch)
    • COVID-19 vaccine
      • Variants of SARS-CoV-2
        • AstraZeneca-Oxford developers say that their vaccine candidate works against the UK variant of SARS-CoV-2. (BBC)

International relations

  • Palau announces its intention to withdraw from the Pacific Islands Forum after claiming fraudulence in the recent election for the organization's Secretary General. (AFP via The Straits Times)

Law and crime

  • One man is killed and 10 others have been injured in a spate of stabbings in south London, England. (BBC)

Politics and elections

  • Second Libyan Civil War, Libyan peace process
    • Delegates of the United Nations-supported Libyan Political Dialogue Forum vote in Geneva to elect Mohammad Younes Menfi as President of the Presidency Council and businessman Mohammed Dbaiba as Prime Minister, defeating the ticket including President of the House of Representatives (Libya), Aguila Saleh, and Interior Minister Fathi Bashagha. (Bloomberg)
