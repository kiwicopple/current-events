Armed conflicts and attacks

  • Insurgency in the Maghreb
    • 2021 Niger border attacks
      • At least 79 civilians are killed and 20 others injured when Islamist militants attack two villages simultaneously in southwestern Niger, near the border with Mali. (Reuters)
  • Mali Civil War, Operation Barkhane
    • Two French Army troops are killed when their armoured personnel carrier hits a roadside bomb in Menaka, Mali. It is the second attack in few days that targeted French troops, since the December 28 JNIM-claimed bombing that killed three French soldiers. (Al Jazeera)

Disasters and accidents

  • 2020 Gjerdrum landslide
    • Rescue workers find the bodies of four people who went missing during a landslide in Gjerdrum, Viken, Norway, on 30 December. Six people are still missing. (BBC)
  • Three people are killed when a small plane crashes into a house in Lyon Township, Michigan. (WDIV-TV)

Health and environment

  • COVID-19 pandemic
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Europe
      • COVID-19 pandemic in the United Kingdom
        • The United Kingdom reports a record 57,725 new cases in the past 24 hours, the highest daily total since the pandemic began. Another 445 deaths from COVID-19 are also reported, bringing the country's total death toll to 74,570. (Sky News)
        • The UK reactivates emergency critical-care COVID-19 hospitals amid a surge in cases and hospitalizations. (Reuters)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Russia, COVID-19 vaccine
        • Over 800,000 people in Russia receive the Sputnik V vaccine. (The Hill)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Gibraltar
        • Starting from 10:00 p.m. local time (21:00 GMT), Gibraltar impose a second lockdown for the next 14 days in order to slow the increase of COVID-19 cases. (AFP via Barron's)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Greece
        • The government announces a one-week closure of all shops that were allowed to reopen during the holiday season, as well as all houses of worship from tomorrow in order to prevent the surge of COVID-19 cases ahead of schools reopening on January 11. A curfew from 9:00 p.m. to 5:00 a.m. local time is to be imposed. (Ekathimerini)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Asia
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Indonesia
        • Indonesia reports a record 7,582 recoveries in the past 24 hours, bringing the nationwide total of recoveries to 625,518. (Kompas)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in South Korea, 501.V2 Variant
        • South Korea reports the first case of a new variant of SARS-CoV-2 when a person who arrived from South Africa on December 26 tested positive for COVID-19. (Dong-A Ilbo)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Vietnam, Variant of Concern 202012/01
        • Vietnam reports the first case of a new variant of SARS-CoV-2 when a 44-year-old woman who arrived from the United Kingdom and later quarantine-tested positive for COVID-19 on December 24. (The Straits Times)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Oceania, COVID-19 vaccine
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Australia
        • Victoria closes its land border with New South Wales amidst a spike in coronavirus cases, after months of open borders. New South Wales meanwhile makes wearing masks in public mandatory, while also easing restrictions for southern suburbs of the Northern Beaches as case numbers there drop. (The Guardian)
      • Palau receives 2,800 doses of the Moderna vaccine in a special contribution from the United States. The government announced that it will vaccinate healthcare workers first and the elderly and people with medical conditions secondly. (RNZ)

Law and crime

  • Capital punishment in Kazakhstan
    • Kazakhstan formally abolishes the death penalty, making a two-decade freeze on executions permanent, according to President Kassym-Jomart Tokayev. (Voice of America)
  • Authorities in Pakistan arrest Zakiur Rehman Lakhvi, alleged leader of the Lashker-e-Taiba group, which is blamed for the 2008 Mumbai attacks that left 166 people dead. (ABC News)
  • The homes of Senate Majority Leader Mitch McConnell and House Speaker Nancy Pelosi are vandalized after failing to pass the CASH Act, a bill that increases the direct payments from $600 to $2,000. (ABC News) (Business Insider)

Politics and elections

  • 2020–21 Nigerien general election
    • The electoral commission of Niger declares that a second round of the presidential election will take place on February 21 after no candidate received a majority of the vote. The two candidates will be former minister Mohamed Bazoum, the leader of the ruling Nigerien Party for Democracy and Socialism, who won the first round with 39.33 percent of the votes, and former president Mahamane Ousmane, who won 16.99 percent of votes. (AFP via Al-Ahram)
