Business and economy

  • Boeing 737 MAX groundings
    • The European Aviation Safety Agency clears the Boeing 737 MAX passenger airliner to return to service into European Union airspace. (AFP via Barron's)
  • GameStop short squeeze
    • A short squeeze causes GameStop stock to jump from $4 to $371.28 after Reddit users predicted a hedge fund closure over the last week. (The Guardian) (AP)

Disasters and accidents

  • Mount Merapi on Java, Indonesia, erupts. No casualties are reported. (The Guardian)

Health and environment

  • COVID-19 pandemic
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Asia
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Indonesia
        • President Joko Widodo receives his second dose of Sinovac Biotech vaccine. (Antara News)
        • Indonesia reports a record 387 new deaths and 10,974 recoveries in the past 24 hours, bringing the nationwide totals to 28,855 deaths and 831,330 recoveries. (detikNews)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Bangladesh
        • Prime Minister Sheikh Hasina launches vaccination programme in Bangladesh using Oxford-AstraZeneca vaccine. (All India Radio)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Myanmar
        • Myanmar begins a vaccination programme using the Oxford-AstraZeneca COVID-19 vaccine. Healthcare workers are the first people to be vaccinated. (AP)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Nepal
        • Prime Minister KP Sharma Oli launches a vaccination campaign against COVID-19 in Nepal starting with 430,000 frontline and healthcare workers. (The Kathmandu Post)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Europe
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Cyprus
        • Cyprus announces a cautious easing of second nationwide lockdown beginning February 1 following decline of COVID-19 cases that peaked after Christmas. (France 24)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in North Macedonia
        • North Macedonia reports the first case of B.1.1.7 variant of SARS-CoV-2 in the sample of a 46-year-old patient that had no travel history and was recovered on January 22. (Xinhua)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Norway
        • Prime Minister Erna Solberg announces the closure of borders for non-essential travel beginning from January 29 in a bid to halt the spread of SARS-Cov-2 variants into Norway. (Forbes)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Morocco
      • Morocco receives half a million doses of Sinopharm vaccine. (Morocco World News)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in South Africa
      • South Africa approves a vaccine developed by University of Oxford and AstraZeneca for emergency use in the country. (Deccan Herald)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in the United States
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Alabama
        • Alabama confirms the first 3 cases of the UK variant of SARS-CoV-2 in two children and one adult in Montgomery and Jefferson counties. (U.S. News & World Report)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in New Jersey
        • New Jersey confirms their first death from the UK variant of SARS-CoV-2 in a person that had "significant underlying conditions." (Patch.com)
        • U.S. Rep. Bill Pascrell of New Jersey's 9th congressional district announces that he will quarantine after making contact with someone who tested positive for COVID-19. (Politico)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Canada
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Ontario
        • A Canada Post worker dies from COVID-19 in Mississauga, Ontario. (Toronto Star)

Law and crime
  • Gretchen Whitmer kidnapping plot
    • One of the fourteen men accused of conspiring to kidnap Michigan Governor Gretchen Whitmer pleads guilty in a federal court. His sentencing is scheduled for July 8. (MLive.com)

Politics and elections

  • Statehood movement in the District of Columbia
    • Democrats in the United States Senate introduce a bill that would make Washington, D.C. become the 51st state. (CBS News)
  • Decommunization in Ukraine
    • In accordance with the Ukrainian decommunization laws, the last statue of Vladimir Lenin in the country (excluding territories currently annexed by Russia or occupied by separatists) is demolished in Odesa Oblast. (Svoboda Radio).
