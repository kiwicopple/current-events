Disasters and accidents

  • Riga hostel fire
    • Eight people are killed and nine others are wounded in a fire at an illegal hostel in Riga, Latvia. (BBC)
  • A 6.4 magnitude earthquake hits the Indian states of West Bengal and Assam, part of Meghalaya, and part of Bangladesh, causing damage to houses and buildings. No casualties have been reported. (India Today) (Dhaka Tribune)

Health and environment

  • COVID-19 pandemic
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Asia
      • COVID-19 pandemic in India
        • COVID-19 vaccination in India
          • India begins a COVID-19 vaccine registration for those aged 18 and above. (The Hindu)
        • India surpasses 200,000 deaths from COVID-19 after a record 3,293 deaths are reported in the past 24 hours. It also reports a new global record of 360,960 new cases, thereby bringing the nationwide total of confirmed cases to 17.99 million. (CNBC)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in the Philippines
        • COVID-19 community quarantines in the Philippines
          • President Rodrigo Duterte approves the extension of the Modified Enhanced Community Quarantine in the Greater Manila Area until May 14. (ABS-CBN News and Current Affairs)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Pakistan
        • Pakistan reports a record 201 deaths from COVID-19 in the past 24 hours, thereby bringing the nationwide death toll to 17,530. (Tribune)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Europe
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Albania
        • Albania surpasses 10,000 vaccinated elderly with the second dose. (TV Klan)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Poland
        • Polish Prime Minister Mateusz Morawiecki announces a easing of its lockdown restrictions beginning May after a fall in new infections, which allow shopping malls DIY and museums to reopen on May 4, hotel can resumes their operation on May 8 and allowing restaurants and cafés to reopen with outdoor terrace on May 15. (The First News)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Turkey
        • Turkey signs an agreement to buy 50 million doses of the Russian Sputnik V vaccine. (Daily Sabah)

International relations

  • 2019–2021 Persian Gulf crisis
    • The United States Navy says that yesterday, three Iranian Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps Navy (IRGCN) patrol boats approached the USS Firebolt at "an unnecessarily close range with unknown intent" and retreated after warning shots were fired. (Fox News)

Law and crime

  • Trump–Ukraine scandal
    • Federal investigators raid the home of Rudy Giuliani, former personal lawyer of former President Donald Trump for suspected illegal lobbying of the federal government on behalf of Ukrainian officials. (The New York Times)
  • Four people are killed and another is wounded during an attack at a hospital in Potsdam, Germany. The perpetrator is arrested. (Reuters)
  • Two children are killed and 16 more wounded during a mass stabbing attack at a kindergarten in Beiliu, China. The attacker is arrested. (BBC)
  • Five former members of the Italian far-left group Red Brigades, the co-founder of Lotta Continua and another far-left militant are arrested in Paris, France. (Ansa)
  • Spanish postal service intercepts a letter with bullets enclosed that has been mailed to former Prime Minister José Luis Rodríguez Zapatero. (El Periódico)

Politics and elections

  • 2021 Northern Ireland riots
    • Northern Ireland First Minister Arlene Foster announces her resignation after losing the support of her Democratic Unionist Party in the aftermath of the riots. (UrduPoint)
