Armed conflicts and attacks

  • War in Afghanistan
    • Withdrawal of U.S. troops from Afghanistan, Afghan peace process
      • U.S. President Joe Biden warns that the country will not meet the May 1 deadline to withdraw from Afghanistan. However, he also said that it is unlikely that troops will remain in the country next year. (Reuters)
    • The Bundestag approves a new mandate to let Germany continue to station up to 1,300 soldiers in Afghanistan until January 31, 2022. (Reuters)

Arts and culture

  • The 2021 redesign of the £50 note, featuring British mathematician Alan Turing on the reverse, is officially unveiled by the Bank of England. It is slated to enter circulation on 23 June, Turing's birthday. (ABC News)

Disasters and accidents

  • Tornado outbreak of March 24–25, 2021
    • Alabama Governor Kay Ivey declares a state of emergency for 46 counties as the National Weather Service predicts a severe weather outbreak and possible tornado outbreak. (WBMA-TV)
    • The National Weather Service issues a particularly dangerous situation tornado watch after level 5 "high risk" watches are issued for parts of Alabama, Mississippi, and Tennessee. (AL.com)
    • A tornado emergency was issued by the National Weather Service near Hoover, Alabama; and then another one for Brent and Centreville. (Alabama WX)
  • 2021 Suez Canal obstruction
    • The Suez Canal Authority suspends navigation through the Suez Canal until the grounded container ship Ever Given can be refloated. The waterway has now been blocked for three consecutive days. (France 24)

Health and environment

  • COVID-19 pandemic
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Asia
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Iraq
        • Iraq receives 336,000 doses of the Oxford–AstraZeneca vaccine distributed through COVAX vaccine sharing initiative. (Arab News)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in the Philippines
        • The Philippines reports a record 8,773 new confirmed cases of COVID-19 in the past 24 hours, thereby bringing the nationwide total of confirmed cases to 693,048. (ABS-CBN)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in South Korea
        • South Korea surpasses 100,000 cases of COVID-19. (Yonhap News Agency)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Thailand
        • Thailand Food and Drug Administration grants the emergency use authorization of single-dose Johnson & Johnson vaccine, becoming the third vaccine to be avaliable for local use. (CNA)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Europe
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Albania
        • Albania receives first batch consisting 192,000 doses of the Sinovac Coronavac vaccine. (Albanian Daily News)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in France
        • The Government of France extends the partial lockdown to three more departments, namely Rhône that includes Lyon, Aube and Nièvre amidst the spike of the COVID-19 infections. The restrictions will begin from midnight tomorrow. (Euronews)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Hungary
        • Hungary reports a record 272 new fatalities of COVID-19 in the past 24 hours, thus bringing the nationwide death toll to 19,224. (Daily News Hungary)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Ukraine
        • Ukraine reports a record 16,669 new confirmed cases and 362 deaths of COVID-19 in the past 24 hours, thus bringing the nationwide total of confirmed cases to 1,596,575 and the nationwide death toll to 31,135. (Ukrinform)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Mexico
      • Mexico surpasses 200,000 deaths of COVID-19. (Reuters)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Peru
      • Peru reports a record 11,260 new confirmed cases of COVID-19 in the past 24 hours amid the spread of the Lineage P.1 variant, thereby bringing the nationwide total of confirmed cases to 1,492,519. (The Rio Times)
    • COVID-19 vaccine
      • Oxford–AstraZeneca COVID-19 vaccine
        • AstraZeneca announces the efficacy rating of its COVID-19 vaccine candidate has been lowered to 76%, following new analyses of the trial run in the United States. Earlier in the week, the establishment had been criticized for advertising the vaccine with a 79% efficacy rating using what critics claimed was outdated data. (Forbes)
      • Pfizer–BioNTech COVID-19 vaccine
        • Pfizer and BioNTech begins a clinical trial testing of its COVID-19 vaccine on healthy 6-month infants to 11-year-old children, which is a crucial step to vaccinating children. (CNBC)

International relations

  • Andorra–Spain relations
    • King Felipe VI of Spain makes the first state visit ever made by a Spanish king to neighboring Andorra. (El Periódico)
  • Aftermath of the 2021 Myanmar coup d'état
    • The United States and the United Kingdom impose sanctions on Burmese conglomerates Myanma Economic Holdings Limited (MEHL) and Myanmar Economic Corporation Ltd (MEC) citing the crackdown and human rights violations committed by the military junta since taking power on last February 1 coup. (Reuters)

Politics and elections

  • 2020–2021 Belarusian protests
    • Exiled opposition leader Sviatlana Tsikhanouskaya calls for a "Freedom Day" and renewed protests against President Alexander Lukashenko to mount pressure for him to resign amidst a disputed election last year. (Reuters)
