Armed conflicts and attacks

  • War in Afghanistan
    • 2021 Afghanistan attacks
      • A car bombing near a police station in Herat Province kills at least eight people and wounds more than 50 others. (Al Jazeera)
  • 2021 Myanmar protests
    • Twelve people are killed after soldiers open fire against protesters in Mandalay, Yangon and Pyay. A 13-year-old child is among the dead, the youngest victim of the unrest. (Reuters)

Arts and culture

  • Avatar retakes its place as the world's highest-grossing film of all time from Avengers: Endgame following a domestic release in China. (CNBC)

Disasters and accidents

  • A Kazakh Air Force Antonov An-26 crashes in Almaty, killing four of six crew members on board, according to the Ministry of Emergency Situations. (Reuters)

Health and environment

  • COVID-19 pandemic
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Europe
      • COVID-19 pandemic in the United Kingdom
        • COVID-19 pandemic in Wales
          • Wales begins to ease their first step of lockdown restrictions, with "stay-at-home" replaced by "stay local". According to new rules, four people from two households can meet outside and outdoor sports can be resumed. (The Daily Telegraph)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Georgia (country)
        • Georgia receives 43,200 doses of the Oxford–AstraZeneca vaccine distributed through COVAX vaccine-sharing initiative. (Agenda.ge)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Hungary
        • Hungary reports a record 9,444 new confirmed cases in the past 24 hours for third day a row, brings the nationwide total of confirmed cases to 507.627. (Hungary Today)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Asia
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Jordan
        • Jordan's health minister resigns after six COVID-19 patients die due to a lack of supplemental oxygen at a hospital ward. (BBC)
        • Jordan receives 144,000 doses of the Oxford-AstraZeneca vaccine distributed through COVAX vaccine-sharing initative. (Roya News)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Mongolia
        • Mongolia reports a record 169 new confirmed cases in the past 24 hours, brings the nationwide total of confirmed cases to 3,833. (Montsame)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in the Philippines
        • The Department of Health reports their first case of Lineage P.1 variant of SARS-CoV-2, in returning Overseas Filipinos who had travelled from Brazil. (Inquirer)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Africa
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Ethiopia
        • Ethiopia begins a vaccination campaign against COVID-19 in order to curb the rising the number of the COVID-19 infections, a week after the country received 2.2 million doses of the Oxford-AstraZeneca vaccine. (Anadolu Agency)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Tunisia
        • Tunisia launches a vaccination campaign against COVID-19 using Sputnik V vaccine that was delivered days ago. Health workers, soldiers, people over 65 and people with underlying condition are first to receive the vaccine. (ABC News)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Australia
      • Australia reports the first local COVID-19 cases in two weeks, when a doctor in Brisbane, Queensland, tested positive for the virus and puts an hospital into a lockdown. (The Independent)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in the United States
      • COVID-19 pandemic in California
        • Over 450 workers at the Tesla Factory in Fremont have tested positive for COVID-19 since Tesla CEO Elon Musk reopened the building last May. (The New York Times)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Brazil
      • Former Brazilian President Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva receives his first dose of the CoronaVac vaccine. (France 24)

International relations

  • China–United Kingdom relations, Hong Kong protests
    • The United Kingdom formally declares China in breach of the 1984 Sino-British Joint Declaration, saying China is now in "a state of ongoing non-compliance" of the treaty. Foreign Secretary Dominic Raab says the UK will "continue to stand up for the people of Hong Kong". (Financial Times)

Law and crime

  • 2020–21 United States racial unrest, Killing of Breonna Taylor
    • Protesters gather in Louisville, Kentucky, on the one-year anniversary of the death of Breonna Taylor. (BBC)
  • 2019 Bolivian political crisis
    • Former interim President Jeanine Áñez is arrested by police in the early hours, accused of participating in an alleged coup d'état against ousted President Evo Morales in November 2019. Before being arrested, Áñez denounced that the "persecution had begun". (BBC)
  • Death of Sarah Everard
    • A Metropolitan Police officer is remanded in custody, to appear at the Old Bailey in London on 16 March. (BBC)
    • A candle-light vigil in Clapham, London, turns violent after crowds of mourners clash with police officers after they attempted to clear the area. Several people are arrested. The police's action draws condemnation from several officials, including London's Mayor Sadiq Khan and Home Secretary Priti Patel. (Evening Standard)

Politics and elections

  • 2021 Western Australian state election
    • The Labor Party wins the election in one of the biggest wipeouts seen in the history of Australia. (ABC Australia)
