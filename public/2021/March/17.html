Armed conflicts and attacks

  • Mali War
    • At least 33 soldiers are killed and 14 others wounded in an attack on a military post in Gao, Mali. (Al Jazeera)

Health and environment

  • COVID-19 pandemic
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Asia
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Kazakhstan
        • Kazakhstan suspends talks with AstraZeneca for purchasing its vaccine amidst claims of severe side-effects in many countries. (Reuters via Yahoo! News)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Lebanon
        • Lebanon suspends the use of the Oxford-AstraZeneca vaccine pending a review from the international health agencies following reports on severe-side effects in vaccinated people. (Naharnet)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in the State of Palestine
        • The Palestinian National Authority receives 61,440 doses of the COVID-19 vaccine, which arrived in Ramallah. 37,440 of the doses are the Pfizer-BioNTech vaccine and the 24,000 others are the Oxford-AstraZeneca vaccine. (The Times of Israel)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Uzbekistan
        • Uzbekistan receives its first consignment consisting of 660,000 doses of the Oxford-AstraZeneca vaccine manufactured by the Serum Institute of India and distributed through COVAX initiative. (AKI Press)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Europe
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Poland
        • The Government of Poland announces a new national lockdown, which will close all cultural venues and all non-essential shops for three weeks from March 20 as COVID-19 cases are on the rise. (Politico.eu)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Ukraine
        • Ukraine reports a record 289 new deaths from COVID-19 in the past 24 hours, thus bringing the nationwide death toll to 28,986. (UNIAN)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in the United States
      • COVID-19 pandemic in New York (state)
        • COVID-19 pandemic in New York City, COVID-19 vaccination in the United States
          • New York Governor Andrew Cuomo receives the Johnson & Johnson vaccine in Harlem. (New York Daily News)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in North Dakota
        • North Dakota confirms their first four cases of the California variant of SARS-CoV-2. Additionally, seven cases of the UK variant is also confirmed. (The Bismarck Tribune)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Brazil
      • Brazil reports a record 90,303 new confirmed cases from COVID-19 in the past 24 hours, thus bringing the nationwide case total to 11,693,838. (Reuters)

International relations

  • Russia–United States relations
    • In an interview published today, President of the United States Joe Biden says President of Russia Vladimir Putin "will pay a price" for reportedly leading efforts to influence the 2020 United States presidential election. Biden also says he believes Putin has no soul and is a killer. (RFE/RL)
    • Russia recalls its ambassador to the United States for consultations, "in order to figure out what to do and where to move in terms of relations with the United States". (TASS)
  • The International Atomic Energy Agency reveals that, in breach of the 2015 nuclear accord between Iran and world powers, Iran has restarted enriching uranium at its underground Natanz facility with a third set of advanced nuclear centrifuges. (Reuters)
  • UNESCO Director General Audrey Azoulay is expected to be reelected as head of the body after no other candidates are nominated. (Reuters)

Law and crime

  • Same-sex marriage in Japan
    • The Sapporo district court rules that prohibiting same-sex marriage is unconstitutional. (Al Jazeera)
  • The United States House of Representatives votes 244-172 to reauthorize the Violence Against Women Act. (NPR)

Politics and elections

  • Politics of Tanzania
    • President of Tanzania John Magufuli dies of heart complications, suspected to stem from COVID-19 infection. Vice President Samia Suluhu, who was Tanzania's first woman VP, should succeed him to become Tanzania's first woman president. (Al Jazeera)
