Health and environment

  • COVID-19 pandemic
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Asia
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Japan
        • Osaka reports 490 new cases in the past 24 hours, a new single-day record since the pandemic began. (Nihon Keizai Shimbun)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Pakistan
        • Pakistan reports 59 new deaths in the last 24 hours, the highest single-day total since July and taking the nationwide death toll to 7,662. (The Times of India)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in South Korea
        • The government will increase social distancing rules in the Seoul Capital Area to Level 2 and both North and South Jeolla Province to Level 1.5 from November 24 amidst a third wave of COVID-19. It means that in level 2, gatherings involving more than 100 are banned, some high-risk facilities are told to close, and restaurants must restrict their dine-in service until 9:00 pm local time. Cafes and coffee shops are only allowed to offer takeout and delivery services. Meanwhile, indoor sports facilities are ordered to close after 9:00 pm. (Yonhap News Agency)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in the United Arab Emirates, COVID-19 testing
        • Dubai Health Authority begins to introduce saliva tests to detect active SARS-CoV-2 infections in children aged 3-to-16 in all DHA facilities and DHA-run screening centers across Dubai. These tests are more than 90% diagnostic accurate, making them a viable alternative to nasal swab tests which, being uncomfortable, make it harder to test children. (Gulf News)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Europe
      • COVID-19 pandemic in France
        • French authorities orders the culling of 1,000 minks in the Eure-et-Loir region after lab analyses detected a mutated version of SARS-CoV-2 in one farm. All animal products in that farm will be eliminated. (The Independent)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Spain, COVID-19 vaccine
        • Prime Minister Pedro Sanchez states that Spain will begin a comprehensive COVID-19 vaccination programme in January and expects to have covered a substantial part of the population within three months. (France 24)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Turkey
        • Turkey reports 6,017 new cases in the last 24 hours, a new record for the second day a row and bringing the nationwide case total to 446,882. (ABC News)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Australia
      • South Australia further eases restrictions imposed earlier in the week, ending a stay at home order but maintaining limits on public and private gatherings and quotas at businesses. (Newshub)
      • New South Wales reopens its border with Victoria as of midnight AEDT after 137-day closure due to spike of the COVID-19 cases in Victoria. (ABC Australia)

International relations

  • 2020 G20 Riyadh summit
    • World leaders at the G20 summit in Riyadh, Saudi Arabia, close the summit with calls for a coordinated response to the COVID-19 pandemic and support for vaccinations for everyone. (The Washington Post) (Reuters)
  • The United States quits the Treaty on Open Skies. (Al Jazeera)

Law and crime

  • Mayfair Mall shooting
    • Several people are arrested, including a 15-year-old boy, in connection with Friday's mass shooting at Mayfair Mall in Wauwatosa, Wisconsin, that injured eight. The shooting followed an altercation between two groups, according to police. (NBC News)

Politics and elections

  • Faisal Mekdad is appointed as Foreign Minister of Syria. Mekdad succeeds Walid Muallem, who died last Monday. (VoA)
