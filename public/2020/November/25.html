Armed conflicts and attacks

  • Libyan Civil War (2014–2020), Libyan peace process
    • The United States Department of the Treasury announces unilateral sanctions on the Libyan National Army linked al-Kaniyat militia and its leader, Mohamed al-Kani, after Russia blocks the United Nations Security Council from blacklisting the group for alleged human rights abuses committed in the city of Tarhuna during the 14-month military offensive led by Field Marshal Khalifa Haftar. (Reuters)

Health and environment

  • COVID-19 pandemic
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Asia
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Bangladesh
        • Foreign Minister AK Abdul Momen and Foreign Secretary Masud Bin Momen test positive for COVID-19 and cancel their visit to Niger for 47th OIC meeting of Foreign Ministers. (Dhaka Tribune)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Indonesia
        • Indonesia reports a record 5,534 new cases in the past 24 hours, bringing the nationwide confirmed cases since the beginning of the pandemic to 511,836. (Tirto.id)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Iran
        • The number of daily confirmed cases in Iran rises by a record 13,843 cases over the last 24 hours, pushing the nationwide total of confirmed cases to 894,385. (Al-Arabiya)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Japan
        • Tokyo Governor Yuriko Koike announces that establishments that serve alcohol and karaoke parlors in the capital will be asked to close by 10:00 p.m. local time for three weeks between November 28 until December 17 as part of measures to curb the spread of COVID-19. The Tokyo Metropolitan Government will provide ¥400,000 in financial compensation to each business that complies with the voluntary request. (The Japan Times)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Malaysia
        • Malaysia reports a single-day record high of 2,348 cases, taking total patients that recovered or discharged to 46,501 or 77.7% of overall cases. (The Edge Markets MY)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Pakistan
        • Pakistan reports 3,009 new cases in the past 24 hours, the highest single-day number since July and taking the total of confirmed cases to 382,892. (The Nation)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Europe
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Croatia
        • Croatia reports a new single-day record of 3,603 new cases, bringing the nationwide total of confirmed cases to 111,617. (The Dubrovnik Times)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Germany
        • Germany reports a record 410 new fatalities in the past 24 hours, according to the Robert Koch Institute, bringing the nationwide death toll to 14,771. (Taggeschau)
        • Chancellor Angela Merkel announces that the partial lockdown will not be lifted until December 20 at the earliest, and may be extended until the beginning of January unless there are unexpected drop in infections. Additional measures also introduced, including requirement of wearing masks in a school and limiting private gathering to five people from two households from December 1. (DW)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Poland
        • Poland reports a record 674 new fatalities in the past 24 hours, including 152 due to COVID-19 alone and 522 with concurrent diseases. The country now has 14,988 total confirmed deaths. (Poland In)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Russia
        • Russia reports a record 507 new fatalities in the past 24 hours, bringing the nationwide death toll to 37.358. (The Moscow Times)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Turkey
        • Turkey registers 28,351 new cases, including 6,814 symptomatic cases. The country now has 467,730 total symptomatic cases. Health Minister Fahrettin Koca stats that for the first time since July, the number of asymptomatic cases will also be reported, which has been criticized for hide the true scale of the outbreak. It also reports a record 168 new fatalities, bringing the nationwide death toll to 12,840. (Reuters)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Canada
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Ontario
        • Ontario reports 35 new fatalities in the last 24 hours, a new single-day record for the second wave of the pandemic and bringing the province's death toll to 3,554 people. (CP24)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Saskatchewan
        • Saskatchewan Premier Scott Moe announces that it will restrict capacities of large stores and other public places to slow the spread of COVID-19. Limits on private gatherings, like weddings and funerals, will also be introduced. (CBC)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in the United States
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Texas
        • Texas reports 14,648 new cases in the last 24 hours, a new single-day record since the beginning of the pandemic. (KTVT)
    • The number of worldwide confirmed cases surpasses 60 million. (RNZ)
  • Avian influenza
    • Poland's local veterinary authorities states that they will cull 930,000 hens in a farm in western Poland due to an H5N8 bird flu outbreak in the village of Wroniawy discovered yesterday, according to state news agency PAP. (NDTV)
    • Japan detects a new case of what is believed to be highly pathogenic strain of avian influenza at a poultry farm in Fukuoka Prefecture, the first case confirmed outside Kagawa Prefecture. As a result, the prefectural government will cull 93,500 chickens at a farm in Munakata to prevent the spread of infections. (The Straits Times)

Law and crime

  • List of people granted executive clemency by Donald Trump
    • President Donald Trump announces via Twitter a pardon of Michael Flynn. (AP)

Politics and elections

  • Politics of Lithuania
    • Ingrida Šimonytė takes office as the new Prime Minister, becoming the second woman to do so. She also appoints the first gender-balanced cabinet in the country's history. (LRT in English)

Sports

  • Belarus at the Olympics
    • The International Olympic Committee announces that they will seek formal punishments for Olympic officials in Belarus for reports of reprisals against athletes during protests against President Alexander Lukashenko. (AP)
