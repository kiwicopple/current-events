Armed conflicts and attacks

  • Tigray conflict
    • Mekelle offensive (2020)
      • Federal forces say they have seized the town of Wukro in Ethiopia's Tigray Region as they advance on the region's capital Mekelle. Tigray People's Liberation Front leader Debretsion Gebremichael confirms that government forces had started an operation to capture Mekelle, saying his forces are "ready to die in defence of our right to administer our region". (BBC)
      • Prime Minister Abiy Ahmed says the Ethiopian military is in "full control" of Mekelle after entering the regional capital with limited resistance and are searching for Tigrayan officials. Chief of General Staff Birhanu Jula Gelalcha says government forces now "completely controlled Mekelle", and that 7,000 Northern Command members being held hostage by the TPLF have been freed. (Al Jazeera)
  • Assassination of Iranian nuclear scientists
    • Assassination of Mohsen Fakhrizadeh
      • Iranian president Hassan Rouhani vows to "respond" to Israel, whom the regime has accused of orchestrating the killing, "at the proper time" (CNBC)
      • The supercarrier USS Nimitz is deployed to the Persian Gulf from its station in Bahrain. The U.S. Navy maintains the ship's deployment was not related to "any specific threat." (Reuters)
  • Boko Haram insurgency
    • Boko Haram jihadists attack a farm in Jere, Borno State, Nigeria, killing at least 40 people. The massacre reportedly occurred during the morning, when most workers were harvesting rice. A search is underway for the militants. (Reuters.uk)

Arts and culture

  • College of Cardinals
    • Wilton Daniel Gregory, Archbishop of Washington, becomes the first African American to earn the rank of cardinal. (The Washington Post)

Disasters and accidents

  • A major search and rescue operation is underway following a collapse at a gold mine in Bindura, Zimbabwe. Around 30 miners remain unaccounted for since the collapse, while six men have emerged alive from the mine. (The Guardian)
  • The city of Nuoro, in the island of Sardinia, Italy, is affected by flooding and subsequent mudflows caused by heavy rains, resulting in the death of at least three people. (BBC)

Health and environment

  • COVID-19 pandemic
    • COVID-19 pandemic in North America
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Canada
        • COVID-19 pandemic in Quebec
          • Quebec reports a new single-day record of 1,480 new cases, bringing the province's total of confirmed cases to 139,643. The province also surpasses 7,000 total deaths from the COVID-19. (CFCF-DT)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in the United States
        • COVID-19 pandemic in California
          • Santa Clara County announces new restrictions on sports, businesses, and traveling to stop the spread of COVID-19. (KNTV)
        • COVID-19 pandemic in Oregon
          • Oregon reports a new single-day record of 1,669 new cases, bringing the total case count to 72,506. (Oregonlive.com)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Europe
      • COVID-19 pandemic in France
        • Non-essential shops and hairdresses in France reopens for the first time since October after the country eases its second nationwide lockdown. (The Guardian)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Greece
        • Greece reports a record 121 new deaths in the past 24 hours, bringing the nationwide death toll to 2,223. (Ekathimerini)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Hungary
        • Hungary surpasses 200,000 total cases of COVID-19. In addition, a record of 152 new deaths also reported in the last 24 hours. (Hungary Today)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Turkey
        • Turkey reports a record 182 new deaths in the past 24 hours, bringing the nationwide death toll to 13,373. The country also reports 30,103 new cases, including asymptomatic cases, a new record since the pandemic after asymptomatic cases are included. (Swiss Info)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Ukraine
        • Ukraine surpasses 700,000 total cases of COVID-19 after a record 16,294 new cases reported in the past 24 hours. (Kyiv Post)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Belarus
        • Belarus reports 1,691 cases, a new single-day record, bringing the total number of cases to 133,324. (Xinhua News Agency)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in the United Kingdom
        • Prime Minister Boris Johnson announces that Nadhim Zahawi will be appointed as the country's vaccine rollout minister. (BBC)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Asia
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Japan
        • Japan reports 2,684 new cases in the past 24 hours, a new single-day record since the beginning of the outbreak. in addition, the number of severe patients reaches a record high of 440. (Nippon.com)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Myanmar
        • Myanmar extends the restrictions for 15 days until December 15, according to announcement made by de-facto leader Aung San Suu Kyi due to rise of COVID-19 cases in the country. The current containment measures due to end on November 30. (Bloomberg)
    • COVID-19 vaccine
      • The first batch of one million doses of Sinopharm's vaccine arrives in Casablanca in the presence of Moroccan officials and a team from the company. Morocco has ordered an initial 10 million doses and is currently conducting Phase III trials of the vaccine. (North Africa Post)
      • The first doses of Pfizer's potential vaccine are flown to the United States from Belgium. (The Hill)
  • Avian influenza, Influenza A virus subtype H5N8
    • South Korea's Agriculture Ministry reports the first case of highly pathogenic H5N8 avian influenza among domestic poultry at a duck farm in the city of Jeongeup, North Jeolla Province. (The Korea Times)

International relations

  • Egypt–South Sudan relations
    • Abdel Fattah el-Sisi visits South Sudan, making him the first President of Egypt to do so. (San Francisco Chronicle)
  • Post-Brexit diplomatic talks
    • Brexit negotiations resume between the United Kingdom and European Union, to come to a free trade agreement. Trade negotiation between the UK and the EU had stalled the previous week after a staff-member of EU Chief Negotiator Michel Barnier tested positive for COVID-19. (AP on MSN)
  • European migrant crisis, France–United Kingdom relations
    • UK Home Secretary Priti Patel announces that an agreement has been reached with France to curb illegal migrant crossings in the English Channel. As part of the measures, French border patrol officers will double patrols on French beaches from next week, while enhancing surveillance, including the increased use of drones to detect migrant boats. (BBC)

Law and crime

  • 2020 Indian farmers' protest
    • Thousands of farmers protest in New Delhi against legislation they say will devastate crop prices. (AP)

Politics and elections

  • Protests against Benjamin Netanyahu
    • Protesters gather at the Beit Aghion residence in Jerusalem to call for the resignation of Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu for his handling of the COVID-19 pandemic in the country and while he is on trial for corruption. (Toronto Star)
  • Aftermath of 2020 Malaysian political crisis
    • Prime Minister Muhyiddin Yasin says that he would call for a general election once the coronavirus pandemic is over due to "difficult and challenging situation" faced by his Perikatan Nasional government which has a slim majority in Parliament. (The Strait Times)
