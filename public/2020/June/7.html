Armed conflicts and attacks
  • Insurgency in Jammu and Kashmir
    • After the Indian Army reports its troops killed five fighters and at least one house is destroyed with explosives in the exchange, violent protests erupt in Kashmir demanding the end of the Indian occupation of, and direct federal rule over, the territory. (Al Jazeera)

Arts and culture
  • George Floyd protests
    • George Floyd protests in the United Kingdom
      • The 1895 statue of Edward Colston is pulled down by demonstrators and thrown into Bristol Harbour, England. (The Guardian)

Health and environment
  • COVID-19 pandemic in Saudi Arabia
    • The total tally of cases in Saudi Arabia exceeds 100,000. (Reuters)

Law and crime
  • Shooting of Iyad Halaq
    • Prime Minister of Israel Benjamin Netanyahu expresses his condolences for the death of an autistic Palestinian man and says he "expects a full investigation into the matter" from Interior Security Minister Amir Ohana. (Reuters)
  • The Philippine Department of Justice probes the proliferation of sockpuppet Facebook accounts that have been using the identities of students and journalists involved in protests against the passage of an anti-terrorism bill. (Bloomberg)

Politics and elections
  • Killing of George Floyd
    • The Minneapolis City Council passes a resolution beginning the process of abolishing the Minneapolis Police Department. (Star Tribune)
