Armed conflicts and attacks

  • Yemeni Civil War
    • 2020 Aden attacks
      • At least 25 people are killed and more than 110 others wounded in an attack at the Aden International Airport, shortly after a plane carrying the newly formed government for Yemen arrived from Saudi Arabia. Prime Minister Maeen Abdulmalik Saeed is taken to safety along with the Saudi ambassador and the rest of the government's members. The Houthis are blamed for the attack, but the group denies responsibility. Another blast is reported later at the presidential palace, where the Saeed cabinet is meeting. (The Washington Post)
  • Syrian civil war
    • Syrian state media reports an ambush on a bus near Palmyra has killed at least 37 people. A senior military defector in the region says the bus was carrying Syrian Army troops and pro-government militiamen from Deir ez-Zor, and was returning to the Tiyas Military Airbase. (Reuters)(SOHR)

Disasters and accidents

  • A landslide in the village of Gjerdrum, Norway, has left at least ten people injured and buried several houses. Eleven people are missing. (BBC)

Health and environment

  • COVID-19 pandemic
    • COVID-19 pandemic in North America
      • COVID-19 pandemic in the United States
        • COVID-19 pandemic in California, Variant of Concern 202012/01
          • California reports its first case of the more contagious variant of SARS-CoV-2 that originated from the United Kingdom. The patient is from Southern California, according to the announcement from Governor Gavin Newsom. (ABC News)
          • California reports a record 442 new deaths on Tuesday, bringing the statewide death toll to 24,987. (The Hill)
        • COVID-19 pandemic in Alabama
          • Outgoing U.S. Senator Doug Jones of Alabama announces he will quarantine at his home after his wife tested positive for COVID-19. (AL.com)
        • COVID-19 pandemic in Colorado, Variant of Concern 202012/01
          • Colorado authorities announce the possible discovery of a second case of the SARS-CoV-2 variant from the United Kingdom in the state. (CNN)
        • COVID-19 pandemic in North Dakota
          • North Dakota records 96 hospitalizations, making it the first time hospitalizations in the state dipped below 100 since September 24. (AP)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Canada
        • COVID-19 pandemic in Ontario
          • Ontario reports a record 2,923 new confirmed cases in the past 24 hours, bringing the total of confirmed cases in the province to 178,831. (CBC)
          • Toronto reports a record of 1,069 cases. (Global News)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Europe
      • COVID-19 pandemic in the United Kingdom
        • COVID-19 vaccination programme in the United Kingdom
          • The United Kingdom approves a vaccine made by the University of Oxford and AstraZeneca for use in the country. Health Secretary Matt Hancock says the rollout will begin on January 4. (BBC)
        • COVID-19 pandemic in Scotland
          • Scotland reports a record 2,045 new cases in the past 24 hours. (The Herald)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in the Czech Republic
        • The Czech Republic reports a record 16,329 new cases in the past 24 hours as the positivity rate reaches a new record 48.5%. (Radio Prague International)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Germany
        • The Robert Koch Institute reports a record 1,129 new deaths in Germany in the past 24 hours, marking the first time the country has surpassed 1,000 daily deaths since the pandemic began. (DW)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in the Republic of Ireland
        • Taoiseach Micheál Martin confirms that the country will close all non-essential retail in a full level 5 restrictions that remain in place at least one month and delay school reopening until January 11. The authorities also agree to extend travel bans to South Africa and the United Kingdom until January 6. (RTÉ)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Malta, Variant of Concern 202012/01
        • Malta reports its first three cases of a new variant of SARS-CoV-2 from the United Kingdom where two are linked to people who arrived on the island from the UK, while a third case is still being investigated. (Times of Malta)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Turkey
        • Turkey receives the first batch of three million doses of vaccine from Sinovac Biotech at Esenboğa International Airport. (Hurriyet Daily News)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Ukraine, COVID-19 vaccine
        • Ukraine signs an agreement to buy up to 1.8 million doses of Sinovac's COVID-19 vaccine CoronaVac. Prime Minister Denys Shmyhal says the first batch of vaccines could arrive in February. (Reuters)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Asia
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Taiwan, Variant of Concern 202012/01
        • Taiwan reports its first case of a new variant of SARS-CoV-2 after a teenager who returned from the United Kingdom on Sunday tested positive for COVID-19 a day later. (Focus Taiwan News Channel)
        • As a result of this case, Taiwan will ban all non-resident foreigners from entering the country starting on January 1. Aside from legal residents, exceptions to the ban will also apply to those who give special permission, such as businesspeople, and the spouses and children of Taiwanese nationals. (Radio Taiwan International)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in India, Variant of Concern 202012/01
        • India extends the suspensions of flights from the United Kingdom until January 7 in order to contain the spread of a new variant of SARS-CoV-2. (Hindustan Times)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Singapore
        • Singapore officially begins its vaccination campaign, with healthcare workers among the first to receive the Pfizer-BioNTech vaccine tozinameran. (CNA)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Thailand
        • Thailand imposes a ban on large gatherings and events in controlled zone across the country in an effort to contain its worst COVID-19 epidemic yet as the authorities reports 250 new cases in the past 24 hours. (Bangkok Post) (CNA)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Argentina
      • Argentina approves a vaccine made by the University of Oxford and AstraZeneca for use in the country, becoming the second country in the world after the UK to approve the vaccine. (France 24)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Zambia, 501.V2 variant
      • Health Minister Chitalu Chilufya confirms Zambia's first case of a new variant of SARS-CoV-2 from South Africa and that a second wave of the pandemic has emerged. (Xinhua)

Law and crime

  • Abortion in Argentina
    • Voluntary Interruption of Pregnancy Bill
      • Argentina legalizes abortion by a Senate vote of 38 to 29, with one abstention. President Alberto Fernández has indicated that he will sign the bill into law. (The New York Times)
  • Islam in Indonesia
    • Indonesia bans any activities held by Islamic Defender Front, the hardline Islamic group led by the controversial cleric Muhammad Rizieq Shihab. The Coordinating Political, Legal, and Security Minister Mahfud MD says the ban is caused by it no longer has legal standing as a mass organisation. (The Straits Times)

Politics and elections

  • Trade negotiation between the UK and the EU
    • The United Kingdom's House of Commons approves the EU–UK Trade and Cooperation Agreement with the European Union by 521 votes to 73. (BBC)
    • Prime Minister Boris Johnson formally signs the agreement into law at Downing Street. (Sky News)
  • 2021 Ugandan general election
    • Bobi Wine, challenger to the incumbent President Yoweri Museveni who has been in office since 1986, is arrested along with his entire campaign team in the town of Kalangala. No information about the arrest has been made available. (CNN via MSN)

Science and technology

  • 2020 in paleontology
    • Scientists at the Russian Academy of Sciences announce the discovery of a "well-preserved" woolly rhinoceros carcass in Abyysky District, Sakha, Russia, which was revealed by melting permafrost in August. The Russian Academy of Sciences says the woolly rhino, was likely 3 or 4 years old when it died by drowning in the river, and is anywhere from 20,000 to 50,000-years-old. (ABC News)
