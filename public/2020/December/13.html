Armed conflicts and attacks

  • Terrorism in Pakistan
    • An IED explodes near a police station in Rawalpindi, Punjab, injuring 25 people. (The News)

Health and environment

  • COVID-19 pandemic
    • COVID-19 pandemic in North America
      • COVID-19 pandemic in the United States
        • COVID-19 vaccine
          • COVID-19 pandemic in Washington, D.C.
            • President Donald Trump, Vice President Mike Pence, and other federal government officials are expected to be among the first to receive the vaccine during the coming days. (USA Today)
          • Pfizer and BioNTech announce that they have started shipping their vaccine from a Michigan facility to all 50 states. (The Wall Street Journal)
        • COVID-19 pandemic in North Dakota
          • North Dakota reports zero deaths from COVID-19, marking it the first time this occurred in a 24-hour period since September 20. (AP)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Canada, COVID-19 vaccine
        • The first doses of the Pfizer-BioNTech vaccine arrives in Canada. (CBC)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Asia
      • COVID-19 vaccine
        • COVID-19 pandemic in Bahrain
          • Bahrain's National Health Regulatory Authority approves the registration of BBIBP-CorV developed by Sinopharm after a data from Phase III clinical trials which the country had participated showed an 86% efficacy rate. (Gulf News)
        • COVID-19 pandemic in Kuwait, COVID-19 vaccine
          • Kuwait's Ministry of Health approves an Emergency Use Authorization of the Pfizer-BioNtech vaccine against COVID-19. (Arab News)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in South Korea
        • The number of new confirmed cases in South Korea reaches the 1,000 mark for the first time since the pandemic began, raising the nationwide total to 42,766. (Yonhap News Agency)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Africa
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Mauritania
        • Mauritania's government reimposes a nighttime curfew in response to a surge in COVID-19 cases that made its hospitals stretch to the limit. (Barron's)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Eswatini
        • Prime Minister Ambrose Mandvulo Dlamini dies at the age of 52 after weeks of hospitalization due to COVID-19. He is the first sitting national head of government to die as a result of the virus. (Swaziland News)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Germany
      • Chancellor Angela Merkel announces that after meeting with leaders of the 16 states, a hard lockdown will begin from December 16 to January 10 2021. It means that non-essential shops and schools will close across the country and New Year's Eve events and the sale of fireworks will be banned, as will the drinking of alcohol in public places. (BBC)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in South America
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Uruguay
        • Uruguay reports a new single-day record of 528 cases, bringing the total number of confirmed cases to 9,708.(gob.uy)

International relations

  • Trade negotiation between the UK and the EU
    • Both parties agree to keep talking, even beyond a deadline they earlier set for today. (CNN)

Law and crime

  • Women in Iran
    • A court in Tehran, Iran, sentences two women's rights activists to a combined total of 15 years in prison. Hoda Amid and Najmeh Vahedi, who were arrested in 2018, stood accused of "collaborating with the United States government against the Islamic Republic on the issue of women and the family". (Al Arabiya English)
