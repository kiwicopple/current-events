Armed conflicts and attacks

  • Nagorno-Karabakh conflict
    • Azerbaijan says that 2,783 Azerbaijani soldiers were killed during the 2020 Nagorno-Karabakh war. (Al Jazeera)
  • Mali Civil War
    • The United Kingdom announces that 300 troops will be deployed to Mali as part of the MINUSMA peacekeeping mission. (The Guardian)

Business and economy

  • Nauru–United States relations
    • The U.S. and Nauru sign a trade deal which is expected to provide access to the full range of investment support offered by the US to the Pacific island nation. Nauru High Commissioner MIchael Aroi thanked U.S. ambassador to several Pacific island countries Joseph Cella for continuing American support to boost relations between both countries. (RNZ)

Disasters and accidents

  • 2020 California wildfires
    • A wildfire breaks out in Orange County, California, U.S., and reaches to over 2,000 acres, prompting evacuations. (Los Angeles Times)
  • Avonmouth explosion
    • Four people are killed and another injured by an explosion at a wastewater treatment works in Avonmouth, Bristol, England, United Kingdom. (BBC)

Health and environment

  • COVID-19 pandemic
    • COVID-19 pandemic in North America
      • COVID-19 pandemic in the United States
        • COVID-19 pandemic in California
          • Californian Governor Gavin Newsom introduces a regional stay-at-home order that require the closure of bars, wineries, hair salons and barbershops for three weeks in a regions where ICU capacity fall below 15% and restrict non-essential travel. The order divides the state into five regions. (CBS News)
        • COVID-19 pandemic in North Dakota
          • North Dakota lawmakers issue a mask mandate for their upcoming legislative season at the state capitol. The measure will take effect on January 5, 2021. (Grand Forks Herald) (U.S. News & World Report)
        • COVID-19 pandemic in Iowa
          • Iowa Governor Kim Reynolds says that the state is expected to receive up to 172,000 vaccine doses this month. (Des Moines Register)
        • President-elect Joe Biden announces that Vivek Murthy and Jeffrey Zients will lead his administration's response to the COVID-19 pandemic. (Politico)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Canada
        • COVID-19 pandemic in Alberta
          • Alberta reports 1,854 cases, a new single-day record. (CBC)
        • COVID-19 pandemic in Quebec
          • Quebec Premier François Legault cancels a plan to allow Christmas gatherings of up to 10 people in the province amid a rise of cases. (The Globe and Mail)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Europe
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Greece
        • The government extends the nationwide lockdown by one week until December 14 to curb the spread of COVID-19. (Ekathimerini)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Italy
        • Italy reports a record 993 new deaths in the past 24 hours, bringing the nationwide death toll to 58,038. It is the highest single-day death toll since March 27. (Corriere della Sera)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Hungary
        • Hungary reports a record 182 new deaths in the past 24 hours, bringing the nationwide death toll to 5,324. (Hungary Today)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Russia
        • The number of new confirmed cases in Russia increases by a record 28,145 new cases over the past 24 hours, pushes the nationwide total of confirmed cases to 2,375,546. (The Moscow Times)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in the United Kingdom
        • The United Kingdom surpasses 60,000 total deaths of COVID-19. (BBC)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Asia
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Indonesia
        • The number of new confirmed cases in Indonesia surges by a record 8,369 new cases over the past 24 hours, pushes the nationwide total of confirmed cases to 557,877. Papua reports more cases than any other province with 1,755 new cases. (Okezone)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Iran
        • Iran surpasses one million total cases of COVID-19. (Bloomberg)
    • COVID-19 vaccine
      • Moderna says that they are expected to have 100-125 million vaccine doses of their vaccine shipped globally during the first quarter of 2021. (Reuters)
  • 2020 H5N8 outbreak
    • South Korea reports a new case of highly pathogenic avian influenza H5N8 on wild birds in Buan, North Jeolla Province according to the Ministry of Agriculture, Food and Rural Affairs. (Yonhap News Agency)

International relations

  • Foreign relations of North Macedonia
    • North Macedonia and the United Kingdom sign a Partnership, Trade and Cooperation Agreement to continue preferential trade terms after the end of the UK's Brexit transition period on December 31, 2020. The agreement also strengthens political, economic, security and cultural ties between both nations. (Gov.uk)

Law and crime

  • 2020 United States racial unrest
    • Kenosha unrest
      • The Kenosha County Circuit Court orders Kyle Rittenhouse, the 17-year old teen accused of shooting three people during protests in Kenosha, Wisconsin, to stand trial for homicide. (ABC News)
  • Hong Kong national security law, Pro-democracy camp (Hong Kong)
    • Hong Kong media tycoon and pro-democracy activist Jimmy Lai is denied bail and remains in custody over alleged fraud related to the lease of a building that houses his Apple Daily, an anti-government newspaper. (Reuters)

Science and technology

  • Misinformation related to the COVID-19 pandemic
    • Facebook announces that it will remove misinformation related to COVID-19 vaccines as countries prepare to distribute them. (CNBC)
  • Chinese space program
    • China National Space Administration profe Chang'e 5 lifts-off the surface of the moon as it begins a journey back to Earth after collecting samples of lunar soil. The ascender will rendezvous with the orbiter, to pass over the samples, which will then land at a location in Inner Mongolia. (BBC)
