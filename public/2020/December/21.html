Armed conflicts and attacks

  • Central African Republic Civil War
    • Rwanda deploys what it calls a "protection force" to the Central African Republic after its peacekeepers were attacked by advancing rebel forces on the capital Bangui. The Rwandan and CAR governments accuse former President François Bozizé of backing the rebels and plotting a coup, which he denies. Russia also sends troops and heavy weaponry to support the government. (BBC)
  • Halle synagogue shooting
    • Germany's Federal Court of Justice sentences the perpetrator of the 2019 synagogue shooting to life imprisonment with subsequent preventive detention. (Reuters)

Arts and culture

  • Removal of Confederate monuments and memorials
    • The statue of Confederate general Robert E. Lee is removed from the United States Capitol; it is slated to be replaced by a statue of civil rights activist Barbara Rose Johns. (The Guardian)

Disasters and accidents

  • Kīlauea volcano on Hawaii erupts prompting the Hawaii County Civil Defense Agency to urge residents to stay indoors. The eruption followed a series of small earthquakes. A previous eruption in May 2018 destroyed hundreds of homes. (CNN)

Health and environment

  • COVID-19 pandemic
    • COVID-19 pandemic in North America
      • COVID-19 pandemic in the United States
        • COVID-19 pandemic in New York (state)
          • COVID-19 pandemic in New York City
            • Governor Andrew Cuomo announces that British Airways will require passengers to test negative for COVID-19 before arriving at the John F. Kennedy International Airport due to the new variant of the SARS-CoV-2 in the United Kingdom. (WNBC-TV)
            • Delta Air Lines announces that it would require passengers from New York to the United Kingdom and elsewhere to conduct COVID-19 tests prior to departure. (U.S. News and World Report)
        • COVID-19 pandemic in Illinois
          • Illinois reports 4,699 cases, the first time the state reported less than 6,000 cases and the lowest since October 27. (WBBM-TV)
        • COVID-19 pandemic in Delaware
          • President-elect Joe Biden and incoming First Lady Jill Biden receive their first shot of a vaccine made by Pfizer and BioNTech on live television to reassure Americans on the safety of vaccines. Meanwhile, Vice President-elect Kamala Harris and incoming Second Gentleman Doug Emhoff will receive their vaccines next week. (CNN)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Canada
        • COVID-19 pandemic in Ontario
          • Premier Doug Ford announces the province-wide lockdown across Ontario from December 26 until January 9 in Northern Ontario and January 23 in Southern Ontario in a bid to curb the spread of COVID-19 in the province. (CBC)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Europe
      • Travel restrictions related to the COVID-19 pandemic
        • COVID-19 pandemic in Norway
          • The government bans direct flights to and from from the United Kingdom due to the emergence of a new variant of the SARS-CoV-2. (NRK)
        • COVID-19 pandemic in Russia
          • Russia announces the suspension of all flights to and from the United Kingdom for one week from midnight due to the new variant of the SARS-CoV-2. (The Moscow Times)
        • COVID-19 pandemic in Portugal, COVID-19 pandemic in Spain
          • The government announce the suspension of all flights from Spain to the United Kingdom and elsewhere from tomorrow, with exception of Spanish citizens or those resident in Spain. The day before, a similar suspension had also been adopted by Portugal. (El Pais in English)
        • COVID-19 pandemic in Sweden
          • The government announces a ban on travel into Sweden from the United Kingdom and Denmark for one month due to the emergence of VUI – 202012/01, a new strain of COVID-19. (Yahoo! News)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Belarus, COVID-19 vaccine
        • Belarus is expected to begin production of Russia's Sputnik V vaccine during the first quarter of 2021 after it become the first foreign country to register that vaccine. (Meduza)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Denmark, Cluster 5
        • The Parliament passes a law that bans mink breeding until 2022. (Reuters)
      • The European Medicines Agency conditionally approves the vaccine made by Pfizer and BioNTech to used across the European Union. (ABC News)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Asia
      • COVID-19 pandemic in India
        • COVID-19 pandemic in Maharashtra
          • Maharashtra will impose a curfew in Mumbai and other major cities at 11:00 pm to 6:00 am IST from tomorrow until January 5 in order to prevent the spread of new variant of SARS-CoV-2 that was discovered in the United Kingdom. The state government also impose 14-day quarantine for anyone from Europe and the Middle East. (Livemint)
        • The government will temporarily suspend flight from India to the United Kingdom due to emergence of a mutated variant of SARS-CoV-2. The suspension will be in effect from tomorrow at 11:59 pm IST until December 31. (Hindustan Times)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in South Korea
        • South Korea reports a record 24 new deaths in the past 24 hours, bringing the nationwide death toll to 698. In addition, the country surpasses 50,000 cases of COVID-19. (Yonhap News Agency)
        • Seoul Metropolitan Government and local governments in Gyeonggi Province and Incheon announce to ban gatherings more than four people in Seoul Capital Area from December 23 to January 3 in order to curb the spread of COVID-19. (The Korea Times) (Al Jazeera)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Hong Kong, VUI – 202012/01, Travel restrictions related to the COVID-19 pandemic
        • Health Secretary Sophia Chan announces that Hong Kong will ban all flights to and from the United Kingdom from tomorrow due to discovery of a more infectious variant of SARS-CoV-2. (South China Morning Post)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Indonesia
        • Indonesia surpasses 20,000 deaths of COVID-19. (Kompas)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Jordan
        • Jordan suspends all flights to and from the United Kingdom until January 3 due to the new mutated variant of SARS-CoV-2. (The Jerusalem Post)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Oman
        • Oman to close land, air and sea borders from tomorrow at 1:00 am local time for one week due to concern over new variant of SARS-CoV-2. (Times of Oman)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Africa
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Egypt
        • Egypt reports a record 664 new cases in the past 24 hours, bringing the nationwide total to 125,555 cases. (Egypt Independent)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Morocco
        • Morocco's government announces to impose a nationwide curfew from 9:00 pm to 6:00 am local time and closure of cafes, shops, supermarkets, and restaurants across the country at 8:00 pm from December 23. In Casablanca, Marrakech, Agadir, and Tangier, restaurants will close for three weeks. (Morocco World News)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Sudan
        • Sudan will ban all travellers from the United Kingdom, the Netherlands, and South Africa from December 23 due to discovery of new variant of SARS-CoV-2. (Reuters)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Australia
      • New South Wales detects a new variant of SARS-CoV-2 from returned travellers from the United Kingdom, where two cases are reported. (The Australian)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Colombia, COVID-19 vaccine
      • Colombia announces that will receive 1.7 million doses of the Tozinameran vaccine. (Reuters)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Antarctica
      • The first cases of COVID-19 in Antarctica are reported. 36 people tested positive for the virus after a week on the Base General Bernardo O'Higgins, in the Chilean Antarctic Territory. (Infobae)

Law and crime

  • Essex lorry deaths
    • Two men are found guilty at London's Old Bailey of the manslaughter of 39 Vietnamese people whom they were smuggling into the UK. The 39 suffocated in a lorry during the journey across the North Sea, from Zeebrugge to Purfleet, in October 2019. (BBC)
  • 2020 Belarusian protests
    • Former Miss Belarus winner Volha Khizhynkova is released from prison after 42 days for protesting against President Alexander Lukashenko. (Zee News)

Politics and elections

  • Poisoning of Alexei Navalny
    • A report surfaces that a Federal Security Service agent allegedly involved in the August plot to poison the Russian opposition leader was "duped" by Navalny into admitting the poison was planted in Navalny's underpants. (CNN)

Science and technology

  • Great conjunction
    • The gas giant planets Jupiter and Saturn appear at their closest in the sky since 1623, in an event known as a great conjunction. The closest approach occurs at 18:22 UTC, when the two planets will be one-tenth of a degree apart; they will appear to be a binary object to the naked eye. (Euronews)
