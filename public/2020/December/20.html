Armed conflicts and attacks

  • War in Afghanistan
    • A car bombing in Kabul targeting Khan Mohammad Wardak, a lawmaker, kills nine people and wounds more than 20 others. Wardak is among the injured. Attacks also occur in Logar, Nangarhar, Helmand, and Badakhshan, with an unknown number of civilians and security forces killed. No group claims responsibility. (Reuters)
  • Eight rockets attack the U.S. Embassy in Baghdad, Iraq. (ABC News)

Business and economy

  • Economic impact of the COVID-19 pandemic in the United States
    • Senate Majority Leader Mitch McConnell announces that Congress has reached a deal on a $900 billion COVID-19 relief package that includes a new round of direct payments and help for jobless Americans, families and businesses struggling in the pandemic. (The New York Times)

Health and environment

  • COVID-19 pandemic
    • COVID-19 pandemic in North America
      • COVID-19 pandemic in the United States
        • COVID-19 pandemic in Georgia
          • Jentezen Franklin, senior pastor of Free Chapel, tests positive for COVID-19. (WXIA-TV)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in El Salvador
        • El Salvador bans travellers who have been in the United Kingdom or South Africa in the last 30 days or whose flights included a layover those countries. (Reuters)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Asia
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Indonesia
        • Indonesia reports a record 221 new deaths in the past 24 hours, bringing the nationwide death toll to 19,880. (detikHealth)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Iran, VUI – 202012/01
        • Iran suspends flights to and from the United Kingdom for two weeks due to the new SARS-CoV-2 variant that emerged in the UK. (Reuters)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Saudi Arabia
        • Saudi Arabia suspends all international passenger flights for one week and entry through land and sea ports in response to a new variant of the SARS-COV-2 that founded in the United Kingdom and other countries. (Arab News)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in South Korea
        • The number of confirmed cases in South Korea reaches a record 1,097 cases in the past 24 hours, 185 of them are from prison, bringing the nationwide total to 49,665 cases. (CNA)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Israel, VUI – 202012/01
        • Israel bans entry of non-citizens from Denmark, South Africa, and the United Kingdom due to emergence of a new variant SARS-CoV-2. Israeli citizens arriving from those countries will be required to enter at state-run quarantine hotels for isolation for up to 14 days. (The Jerusalem Post)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Qatar, COVID-19 vaccine
        • The Ministry of Public Health approves the authorization of Pfizer-BioNTech vaccine for emergency use in Qatar. (Gulf Times)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Europe
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Bulgaria, VUI – 202012/01
        • The Bulgarian government announces the suspension of all flights between Bulgaria and the United Kingdom from tomorrow until January 31. Anyone arrived from the UK are subjected to mandatory 10-day quarantine. (Sofia Globe)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Greece
        • Greece introduces 7-day quarantine for arrivals from the United Kingdom from tomorrow at 6:00 am local time. (Greek Travel Pages)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in the Netherlands, VUI – 202012/01
        • The Dutch government orders a passenger flight ban from the United Kingdom from 6:00 am today until at least January 1 due to the spread of a new variant of SARS-CoV-2 in the UK, with a case of the variant already being reported in the Netherlands. (The Independent)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in the United Kingdom
        • The United Kingdom reports a record 35,928 new cases in the past 24 hours. The government data also shown that 90% local authorities in England have reported a rise in cases in the last week. (The Daily Telegraph)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Belgium, VUI – 202012/01
        • Belgium bans entry for incoming rail and air passengers from the United Kingdom for at least 24 hours, starting next midnight. Virologist Marc Van Ranst said yesterday that the virus variant had been found in four cases from a sample of 2,000 analyses in his laboratory over the past months. (VRT)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Turkey, VUI – 202012/01
        • Turkey temporaily suspend flights from the United Kingdom, the Netherlands, Denmark and South Africa due to concern over a new variant of SARS-CoV-2. (Daily Sabah)
      • European Union response to the COVID-19 pandemic, VUI – 202012/01
        • At least 8 more EU countries set travel restrictions with the UK. Germany calls an emergency meeting of the European Council tomorrow. (Euronews)

Politics and elections

  • President of Nepal Bidya Devi Bhandari and Prime Minister KP Sharma Oli dissolve the incumbent House of Representatives, the lower house of the Nepali Parliament. (The New York Times)
