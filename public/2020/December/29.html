Business and economy

  • Economic impact of the COVID-19 pandemic in the United States
    • Senate Majority Leader Mitch McConnell blocks an attempt by Senate Democrats to increase the direct payments from $600 to $2,000. However, McConnell also said that the Senate will consider a process to provide $2,000 in stimulus checks to Americans, a measure supported by Democrats and President Donald Trump. (NBC News) (Business Insider)
  • The first commercial Boeing 737 MAX flight in the United States since worldwide groundings began in March 2019 is held. The flight, between New York City's LaGuardia Airport and Miami International Airport, was provided by American Airlines. (CNBC)

Disasters and accidents

  • 2020 Petrinja earthquake
    • An earthquake of magnitude 6.4 strikes Petrinja, Croatia, destroying half of the town. Several cities in the country are also affected. At least seven people are killed, five of whom in Glina, one in Petrinja and one in Žažina, and several others injured. (BBC)
    • The Krško Nuclear Power Plant in neighboring Slovenia, which provides for around 20 percent of the country's electricity, shuts down as a precaution. (AFP via NDTV)

Health and environment

  • COVID-19 pandemic
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Europe
      • COVID-19 pandemic in the United Kingdom
        • COVID-19 pandemic in Northern Ireland
          • Northern Ireland reports a record 1,566 new cases in the past 24 hours, bringing the total of confirmed cases in the country to 68,762. (RTÉ)
        • Nationwide, a record 53,135 new cases are reported in the United Kingdom in the past 24 hours, as tougher measures eyed to fight a new variant. (ABC News)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in the Republic of Ireland
        • Ireland reports a record 1,546 new cases in the past 24 hours, bringing the nationwide total of confirmed cases to 88,439. (The Irish Times)
        • Ireland officially begins a vaccination programme against COVID-19 Pfizer-BioNTech's tozinameran vaccine, with a 79-year-old woman from Dublin becoming the first person to receive the vaccine. (RTÉ)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Belarus, COVID-19 vaccine
        • Belarus begins rolling out Russia's Sputnik V vaccine. (The Moscow Times)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Denmark
        • Denmark extends a hard lockdown until January 17 due to drastic increase of the case and hospitalizations in the country. (The Straits Times)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in North America
      • COVID-19 pandemic in the United States
        • COVID-19 pandemic in Washington, D.C.
          • Vice President-elect Kamala Harris and her husband Doug Emhoff receive the Moderna vaccine. (CBS News)
          • President-elect Joe Biden announces that three more officials will join his COVID-19 Advisory Board. (Politico)
        • COVID-19 pandemic in Colorado
          • Colorado reports the United States first case of new variant of SARS-CoV-2 from the United Kingdom in a male in his 20s who is in isolation in Elbert County and has no travel history to the UK. (The Washington Post)
        • COVID-19 pandemic in Kentucky
          • Kentucky Governor Andy Beshear announces that the state will extend their mask mandate for 30 days after reporting 2,990 new cases of COVID-19. (WKYT-TV)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Canada
        • COVID-19 pandemic in Ontario
          • Ontario reports a record 2,553 new confirmed cases in the past 24 hours, along with 1,939 new cases yesterday, bringing the total of confirmed cases in the province to 175,908. (Global News)
        • COVID-19 pandemic in Quebec, Variant of Concern 202012/01
          • Quebec reports the first of a new variant of SARS-CoV-2 in a person who had been in contact with a family member who returned from the United Kingdom on December 11 and tested positive for COVID-19 on December 13. (Montreal Gazette)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Asia
      • COVID-19 pandemic in South Korea
        • South Korea reports a record 40 new deaths in the past 24 hours, bringing the nationwide death toll to 859. (Al Jazeera)
        • United States Forces Korea begins a vaccination programme against COVID-19 using Moderna vaccine. Healthcare providers and first responders are among those who receive the vaccine. (Stars and Stripes)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in India, Variant of Concern 202012/01
        • India reports six cases of the more contagious variant of SARS-CoV-2 from individuals arrived from the United Kingdom. All of them are in isolation. (BBC)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Japan, 501.V2 variant
        • Japan reports the first case of new variant of SARS-CoV-2 from South Africa when a 30-year old woman who arrived in Japan on December 19 tested positive for COVID-19. (The Japan Times)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Pakistan, Variant of Concern 202012/01
        • Pakistan reports the first case of new variant of SARS-CoV-2 from the United Kingdom when six people out of 12 passengers who returned from the UK in Sindh tested positive for COVID-19, therefore three people are showed the new variant. (The Hindu)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in the Philippines
        • The Philippines extends its travel ban to 19 "flagged" countries and territories from midnight until January 15 in order to prevent new variants of SARS-CoV-2 from entering the country. (CNA)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Oceania
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Australia, 501.V2 variant
        • Queensland reports Australia's first case of a new variant of SARS-CoV-2 from South Africa when a woman who arrived to the state on December 22 tested positive for COVID-19. (ABC Australia)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Samoa
        • The government of Samoa halts travel from the United Kingdom and South Africa in response to the new variant of the virus SARS-CoV-2 discovered in those countries. (RNZ)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in South America
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Argentina, COVID-19 vaccine
        • Argentina begins a vaccination campaign with the Russian Sputnik V vaccine. (Europa Press)
      • COVID-19 pandemic in Chile, Variant of Concern 202012/01
        • Chile reports the first case of a new variant of SARS-CoV-2 in a resident of Panguipulli who arrived on December 23 from Madrid. (La Tercera)

International relations

  • United Kingdom–Vietnam relations
    • The United Kingdom and Vietnam sign a free trade agreement, to take effect on December 31, following a meeting between UK Foreign Secretary Dominic Raab and his Vietnamese counterpart Pham Binh Minh at the State Guest House in Hanoi, Vietnam. (Reuters)

Law and crime

  • Shooting of Tamir Rice
    • The United States Department of Justice announces that there will not be any charges against the two Cleveland officers accused of killing Tamir Rice. (NBC News)
  • The Investigative Committee of Russia accuses opposition leader and anti-corruption activist Alexei Navalny of fraud for allegedly using public funds of his organization on personal needs. The accusation comes after the federal prison service demanded Navalny to report to its office today or face jail time. (DW)

Politics and elections

  • Abortion in Argentina
    • Voluntary Termination of Pregnancy Bill
      • The Senate begins to debate the legalization of abortion up until the 14th week of pregnancy. If passed, Argentina will become the third Latin American country to allow abortion to be performed on demand, after Cuba and Uruguay. The Catholic Church opposes the move. The bill has passed the lower house. (Reuters)
