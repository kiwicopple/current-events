Armed conflicts and attacks

  • Russo-Ukrainian War
    • 2022 Russian invasion of Ukraine
      • Foreign aid to Ukraine during the Russo-Ukrainian War
        • The United Kingdom's Armed Forces Secretary James Heappey says Ukraine can strike targets inside Russia with British weapons, saying it was "completely legitimate" in order to disrupt attacks on Ukraine. (The Guardian)
        • The Russian Defence Ministry accuses the UK of "provoking Ukraine" into attacking Russian territory, and warns of a "proportional response" should any attacks occur. Russia also says it will target "decision-making centres" in Kyiv. (BBC News)
      • 2022 Russia–Ukraine peace negotiations
        • UN Secretary General António Guterres visits Moscow to meet with Russian President Vladimir Putin. Before the meeting, he calls for a ceasefire to end the war. (The Hindu)
      • A Russian missile strike destroys a strategic bridge across the Dniester Estuary in Odessa Oblast, according to Ukrainian authorities. (AP)
      • Russian Foreign Minister Sergey Lavrov says that Western weapons supplied to Ukraine "will be a legitimate target for Russia's military acting within the context of the special operation" and referred to "storage facilities in western Ukraine have been targeted more than once [by Russian forces]". He states that "NATO, in essence, is engaged in a war with Russia through a proxy and is arming that proxy. War means war." (Al Jazeera) (The Independent)
  • Transnistria conflict
    • Moldova's Supreme Security Council convenes an emergency meeting after yesterday's attacks in the breakaway Transnistria region. President Maia Sandu says the attacks were aimed at "destabilising the situation in the region", and says the military will improve combat readiness and increase patrols on the border with Transnistria. (Reuters)
  • 2022 University of Karachi bombing
    • Three Chinese citizens and their Pakistani driver are killed and four other people are wounded when a Balochistan Liberation Army suicide bomber blows herself up near their van in Karachi, Sindh, Pakistan. (BBC News) (Reuters)
  • Insurgency in the Maghreb
    • A police officer is killed by militants in Karimama, Benin. (Reuters)

Business and economy

  • Russia in the European energy sector
    • 2022 Russia–European Union gas dispute
      • Gazprom announces it will stop delivering natural gas to Poland via the Yamal–Europe pipeline and to Bulgaria as both countries rejected Russia's demand to pay for the fossil fuel supply in Russian rubles. (Deutsche Welle)
        • Poland does not expect to experience disruptions due to its natural gas storage facilities having 40-180 days of supply, the Poland–Lithuania gas pipeline becoming operational next month, and the Baltic Pipe natural gas pipeline from Norway becoming operational this October, which will make Poland completely independent of Russian gas. Poland can also import gas via the Świnoujście LNG terminal. (Rzeczpospolita)
        • The halting of Russian gas to Bulgaria "poses a serious challenge to the security of supply to the country" as Bulgaria is almost completely dependent on Russian gas, with the country importing over 90% of its natural gas from Russia via the Turk Stream pipeline under a 10-year contract that is set to expire at the end of 2022. The country had previously stated in March that this contract will not be renewed due to Russia's invasion of Ukraine. The Gas Interconnector Greece-Bulgaria pipeline will become operational this September and allow Azerbaijan to supply Bulgaria with 1 billion cubic meters (bcm) of the 3 bcm of natural gas it consumes annually. (Reuters)
