Armed conflicts and attacks

  • Russo-Ukrainian War
    • 2021–2022 Russo-Ukrainian crisis
      • War in Donbas
        • The Donetsk People's Republic and Luhansk People's Republic order mandatory mass evacuations of civilians from their respective capital cities, Donetsk and Luhansk. Around 700,000 people are expected to be evacuated to Russia with the first buses carrying civilians to the Russian border already en route to Rostov Oblast. Russian President Vladimir Putin orders lump-sum allowances of 10,000 rubles (129.4 US dollars) to be paid to refugees from Donbas. (MSN) (TASS)
        • A UAZ-469 jeep is blown up outside of a building of the Donetsk People's Republic government in Donetsk city. Nobody is reported to have been hurt in the explosion (Reuters)
        • Russia confirms the Druzhba pipeline is on fire in rebel-held Luhansk Oblast after a massive explosion. The pipeline is one of Russia's main pipelines used to transport oil to the European Union. (Reuters)
        • More explosions are reported in Luhansk. LPR authorities say a gas station has been blown up. (Metro) (National Post)
      • Reactions to the 2021–2022 Russo-Ukrainian crisis
        • The British government relocates its embassy from Kyiv to the western Ukrainian city of Lviv, and again urges its citizens to leave the country while they still can. (Reuters)
        • Estonia delivers a shipment of U.S.-made FGM-148 Javelin anti-tank missiles to Ukraine. (Reuters)
        • U.S. President Joe Biden says Vladimir Putin has now decided to launch a full-scale invasion of Ukraine and will do so in the "coming days". Biden says Russian troops will try to capture the capital Kyiv. (Reuters) (Mirror)
  • Iran–Israel proxy conflict
    • Israel's Iron Dome fails to intercept a Hezbollah-operated military drone from Lebanon that penetrated seventy kilometers into Israeli airspace; the drone flew for forty minutes before returning to Lebanon. Israeli jets fly at very low altitude over Beirut in response. (Times of Israel) (ABC News)
  • Mali War
    • Mali's ruling military junta demands French forces leave the country "without delay" after French President Emmanuel Macron announced a withdrawal of troops in a "orderly fashion" over the coming months. (France 24)
  • Piracy in the Gulf of Guinea
    • Denmark recalls its frigate HDMS Esbern Snare from the Gulf of Guinea following a request from NATO to increase readiness and contribute to deterrence in Europe. The warship has been conducting anti-piracy measures in the Gulf since October 2021. (The Local)

Disasters and accidents

  • 2021–22 European windstorm season
    • At least nine people are killed, including four people in the Netherlands, three in the United Kingdom, and one person in both Belgium and Ireland as Storm Eunice strikes northwestern Europe. (BBC) (Dutch News)
    • A gust of 122 mph (196 kph) is recorded on the Isle of Wight in the English Channel, it is the strongest wind ever recorded in England. (Reuters)
    • The Grain Power Station in Kent, England, goes offline following the collapse of one of its towers. (ITV News)
    • The roof of The O2 Arena in London is severely damaged due to high winds. (BBC)
  • Eleven people are missing after a ferry catches fire between Greece and Italy. (Reuters)

International relations

  • India–United Arab Emirates relations
    • India and the United Arab Emirates sign a free trade agreement over digital goods, raw materials, and apparels. It is the first major trade deal signed by India since Prime Minister Narendra Modi came into power. (Moneycontrol)
  • Poland–United States relations
    • The U.S. Secretary of Defense Lloyd Austin announces the sale of 250 M1 Abrams main battle tanks to Poland amid tensions with Russia. The Polish Land Forces will become the first European military to operate the American M1 Abrams.(Reuters)

Law and crime

  • Canada convoy protest
    • Ottawa Police Service make over 100 arrests, including organizer Pat King. Police accuse protestors of assaulting officers, including one who was arrested after allegedly throwing a bicycle at a mounted officer. (CBC News) (Canadian Press via MSN.com)
