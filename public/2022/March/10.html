Armed conflicts and attacks

  • Russo-Ukrainian War
    • 2022 Russian invasion of Ukraine
      • Eastern Ukraine offensive
        • Siege of Mariupol
          • Russian forces resume shelling the port city of Mariupol after yesterday's airstrike on a children's hospital killed three people. A humanitarian aid convoy trying to reach the city is also forced to turn back due to heavy fighting. (BBC News)
  • An armed incident occurs between border guards at the Kyrgyzstan–Tajikistan border, leaving one Tajik border guard dead. Following the incident, officials from the Batken Region in Kyrgyzstan and the Sughd Region in Tajikistan holds talks. (Trend) (RFE/RL)

Business and economy

  • 2022 Russian invasion of Ukraine
    • 2022 Russian financial crisis
      • Kremlin spokesman Dmitry Peskov says the Russian economy was experiencing a "shock" following an "absolutely unprecedented" economic war being waged against the country. Peskov says "The economic war that has started against our country has never taken place before. So it is very hard to forecast anything". (Reuters)
    • 2022 boycott of Russia and Belarus
      • Video game industry giants Nintendo and Sony Interactive Entertainment's PlayStation both suspend all product sales in Russia. The online PlayStation Store will also be no longer available in Russia. (CNBC)
      • U.S. bank Goldman Sachs announces that it is closing its operations in Russia, becoming the first major Wall Street bank to exit the country following its invasion of Ukraine. (Reuters)
    • International sanctions during the Russo-Ukrainian War
      • Russian President Vladimir Putin admits that the sanctions have created problems for Russia. However, he also called the sanctions illegitimate and warned the West that Russia will emerge stronger and solve the problems. (Reuters) (Sky News)
    • The Ukrainian parliament is debating a new law allowing forced seizure of all assets belonging to Russians and to Ukrainians deemed by the courts or the National Security and Defense Council of Ukraine to be Russian collaborators. A week ago, the Verkhovna Rada approved a bill that allowed the same procedure for assets owned by the Russian government or by its companies. (Ekonomichna Pravda)
    • Russia's Ministry of Transport prepares a draft regulation which says that the airlines will not have to honour the request of the lessor to return leased aircraft unless a special government commission tells them to do so and will also allow companies to pay the lessor in rubles. This comes amid the EU sanctions which force all lease contracts for Russian aircraft to be voided by late March and forbids EU companies to insure Russian aircraft. (Interfax Russia)
    • Russia introduces retaliatory sanctions against all foreign countries, with the exception of the countries in the Eurasian Economic Union (a group of countries that apart from Russia itself, also includes the post-Soviet states of Armenia, Belarus, Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan as full members with Moldova, Uzbekistan and the non-Soviet state of Cuba as observers), Abkhazia and South Ossetia (recognised by Russia but not internationally, which considers both the territories as part of Georgia). Export of more than 200 items, mostly of technical and agricultural appliances, will be forbidden until the end of 2022. (Interfax Russia)
    • By a government decree from 6 March, Russia abolishes penalties for patent theft if the patents are held by natural or legal persons from countries that the government of Russia considers "unfriendly". The government is also considering decriminalising software piracy if the software comes from these countries. (Euractiv) (The Register)
  • Facebook and Instagram will begin to allow users in select countries to call for violence against Russians and Russian soldiers in the context of the Ukraine invasion, which are normally prohibited. Still prohibited are calls for violence against Russian prisoners of war and "credible calls for violence against Russian civilians". It is also allowing users to post death threats against Russian President Vladimir Putin and Belarusian President Alexander Lukashenko. Meta Platforms also says that they are, "for the time being, making a narrow exception for praise of the Azov Regiment strictly in the context of defending Ukraine, or in their role as part of the Ukraine National Guard", which are normally prohibited. (Reuters)

Disasters and accidents

  • Around 250 people evacuate after Mount Merapi in Indonesia erupts overnight. Around 193 residents of Kalitengah later return to the area, which is located near Yogyakarta. (The Washington Post) (Antara)

Health and environment

  • COVID-19 pandemic
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Niue
      • Niue reports its first COVID-19 case since the pandemic began in a passenger arriving on an incoming flight from New Zealand who had tested negative upon departure. (The Guardian)
    • COVID-19 pandemic in the United States
      • COVID-19 pandemic in North Dakota
        • Governor Doug Burgum announces that North Dakota will shift its COVID-19 approach from a pandemic to an endemic phase. The state health department will also end their COVID-19 updates. (AP) (KVRR)
      • Face masks during the COVID-19 pandemic in the United States
        • The Transportation Security Administration and Biden administration will extend the federal mask mandate for public transportation such as transits, planes, trains, buses and airports until April 18. (CNN)
    • COVID-19 vaccine
      • Moderna COVID-19 vaccine
        • Moderna begins a study and a phase 2 trial of a hybrid vaccine aimed at combating the Omicron variant with their current COVID-19 vaccine. (MarketWatch) (BNN Bloomberg)

International relations

  • Colombia–United States relations, Qatar–United States relations
    • U.S. President, Joe Biden, designates Qatar as a Major non-NATO ally and announces his intention to designate Colombia with the same status. (The Jerusalem Post)
  • Russia–Ukraine relations
    • Russian Foreign Minister Sergey Lavrov and Ukrainian Foreign Minister Dmytro Kuleba hold talks in Ankara. These are the highest level talks between the two nations since the invasion. No ceasefire is reached, but the possibility of a meeting with Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy and Russian President Vladimir Putin was discussed. (Reuters)
    • Ukrainian Defense Minister Oleksii Reznikov says that Ukraine is willing to accept Russia’s surrender “with understanding”. (Ukrinform)
  • Russia in the Council of Europe
    • Russia announces it will quit the Council of Europe. (The Moscow Times) (TASS)

Law and crime

  • Judiciary of Poland, Polish constitutional crisis
    • The Constitutional Tribunal, the highest court in Poland, declares that the European Court of Human Rights (ECHR) may not control the validity of appointment of any judges in Poland. The Tribunal also wrote in its opinion that Poland is not obliged to implement four ECHR's court rulings which found that the parties were denied a right to fair trial, as the relevant provision violates the Constitution of Poland when applied to control the judges' appointment. (Rzeczpospolita) (Euronews)

Politics and elections

  • 2022 Hungarian presidential election
    • Fidesz's Katalin Novák is elected the first female president of Hungary by the National Assembly. (Bloomberg)
  • 2022 South Korean presidential election
    • Conservative opposition leader Yoon Suk-yeol is confirmed as the winner of yesterday's presidential election with 48.59% of the vote. He will take office as President of South Korea on May 10. (BBC News)

Sports

  • Reactions to the 2022 Russian invasion of Ukraine
    • International sanctions during the Russo-Ukrainian War
      • The British government sanctions Russian oligarch and Chelsea F.C. owner Roman Abramovich, freezing all his assets in the United Kingdom. The sanctions mean that Premier League club Chelsea will not be able to sell any more tickets for games, its merchandise store will be shut, and it will be unable to buy or sell players on the transfer market until the club is sold. (BBC News)
  • 2022 Major League Baseball season
    • 2021–22 Major League Baseball lockout
      • The Major League Baseball reaches a labor collective bargaining agreement with the the Major League Baseball Players Association to end the lockout and salvage a 162-game season. Opening day will begin on April 7, and training camp will begin around March 11. (ESPN)
