Armed conflicts and attacks

  • Russo-Ukrainian War
    • 2022 Russian invasion of Ukraine
      • Kyiv offensive
        • Battle of Kyiv
          • The Ukrainian Air Force says it has destroyed two Russian warplanes in a dogfight over Kyiv. A Ukrainian MiG-29 is also shot down during the air battle. (Sky News)
      • Kherson offensive
        • Battle of Kherson
          • Russian Ground Forces say they have captured the Black Sea port of Kherson. The Ukrainian Ministry of Defense denies the fall of the city, saying the battle is ongoing. (CNN)
          • The mayor of Kherson surrenders the city to the invading Russian forces. (New York Times)
        • Siege of Mariupol
          • The mayor of Mariupol, Donetsk Oblast, reports mass casualties and a water outage from "non-stop" Russian bombardments. (Reuters)
      • Horlivka offensive (2022)
        • Ukrainian forces begin an offensive toward Horlivka. (Ukrinform)
      • Siege of Chernihiv
        • Two Kalibr cruise missiles strike a hospital in Chernihiv after Russian troops were forced to retreat. (TIME) (CBC)
      • Battle of Kharkiv
        • Russian forces launch airstrikes on Kharkiv National University and a local police department. (CNN)
      • Siege of Enerhodar
        • Russia says its troops have captured the Zaporizhzhia Nuclear Power Plant, the largest nuclear power plant in Europe. The International Atomic Energy Agency confirms Russia had communicated that the plant was under their control and that personnel at the plant were continuing to perform their standard duties, as normal. (The Drive)
      • A missile strikes a Bangladeshi-flagged cargo ship Banglar Samriddhi docked at the Port of Olvia in Mykolaiv Oblast, killing one Bangladeshi engineer. The Bangladesh Shipping Corporation is confirmed as the owner of the vessel. (The Daily Star)
      • The emergency special session of the UN General Assembly adopts a resolution calling for Russia to end the invasion. (The Washington Post) (The Guardian)
      • The European Court of Human Rights, in an unprecedented move, halts all judicial proceedings against Ukraine pending before the ECHR. (Europeiska Pravda)

Business and economy

  • 2022 Russian financial crisis
    • The European Union excludes seven Russian banks from the SWIFT financial messaging system, including Russia's second-largest bank VTB Bank, Novikombank, Promsvyazbank, Rossiya Bank, Sovcombank and VEB.RF. (Reuters)
    • Russian stocks endure a total wipeout in value on the London stock exchange, losing $570 billion in two weeks, with many Russian companies' stocks worth pennies. Entities such as Gazprom and Lukoil have lost almost all value; Sberbank, the largest bank in Russia, had a market cap of $102 billion half a year ago but now the value of outstanding shares is less than $190 million. (Bloomberg) (Barron's)
    • The World Bank suspends all programs in Russia and Belarus in response to Russia's invasion in Ukraine. (Le Temps)

Disasters and accidents

  • A Romanian Air Force MiG-21 from the 86th Air Base, carrying out an air patrol mission over Dobrogea vanishes from radar, and a IAR 330 rescue helicopter sent on a search and rescue mission also disappears. A Defence Ministry spokesman later confirms that the helicopter has crashed near Gura Dobrogei, killing all seven crew members on board. The fate of the MiG-21 and its pilot is still unknown. (Reuters)

Health and environment

  • COVID-19 pandemic
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Australia
      • COVID-19 vaccination in Australia
        • The Australian Technical Advisory Group on Immunisation gives Novavax the green light for Australia to approve the booster shot for their vaccine. (Sky News Australia) (RACGP)
    • A study published by the New England Journal of Medicine shows that the booster shot of the Pfizer–BioNTech COVID-19 vaccine restores protection against the Omicron variant. (NBC News)

International relations

  • Reactions to the 2021–2022 Russo-Ukrainian crisis, International reactions to the 2022 Russian invasion of Ukraine
    • The UNGA condemns Russia for the invasion, and asks to withdraw its troops immediately. (Al Jazeera)
    • Russia–Ukraine relations
      • Ceasefire talks between Russia and Ukraine, scheduled to take place today, are postponed. (TASS)
      • Russian delegation arrives in Belarus for a second round of talks expected to occur on Thursday morning. The Ukrainian delegation is expected to follow suit after leaving Kyiv. (CNN) (News.am)
    • Russia–Turkey relations
      • Turkey says the Russian Navy has cancelled the transit of four of its warships through the Bosporus strait upon its request after triggering the wartime clause of the Montreux Convention Regarding the Regime of the Straits, which allows Turkey to close the straits of Bosporus and Dardanelles to foreign warships. (Reuters)
    • Russia–European Union relations
      • The European Union will ban deliveries of euro banknotes to Russia starting from 12 March, with exemptions for private persons and their family members in Russia as well as diplomatic missions that have legal immunity. (TASS)
    • Russia–United States relations
      • Pentagon Press Secretary John Kirby announces that the U.S. will cancel and postpone a Minuteman III ICBM missile test in a effort to de-escalate tensions between Russia and Ukraine. (Reuters)
    • Germany–Russia relations
      • Germany seizes Alisher Usmanov's superyacht Dilbar in Hamburg after the Russian oligarch was sanctioned by the EU on Monday. (Forbes)
  • Nuclear program of Iran
    • During a meeting in Israel, German Chancellor Olaf Scholz says that a new agreement to revive the Joint Comprehensive Plan of Action "cannot be postponed any longer". However, Prime Minister Naftali Bennett calls the agreement "unacceptable" as he indicated that Iran would be allowed to install centrifuges on a large scale. (Times of Israel)

Law and crime

  • Protests against responses to the COVID-19 pandemic
    • COVID-19 anti-lockdown protests in New Zealand
      • Anti-lockdown rioters clash with police at the New Zealand Parliament Buildings. A fire breaks out, destroying a children's playground. Several police officers are injured and 38 protesters are arrested. (The Guardian) (Stuff)
  • Russo-Ukrainian War
    • The International Criminal Court announces that they have opened a criminal investigation into war crimes that have been committed in Ukraine since 2013. (Business Insider)
  • 2021–2022 Myanmar insurgency
    • The Myanmar military junta pardons several celebrities who spoke out against the military coup including Lu Min, Wyne, Pyay Ti Oo, his wife Eaindra Kyaw Zin, and Paing Takhon. (ABC News)

Science and technology

  • Censorship of Wikipedia
    • Russia's communications regulator, Roskomnadzor, threatens to block the Russian Wikipedia for hosting the article on the Russian invasion on Ukraine, which the regulator alleges to be factually inaccurate. (Reuters)
  • OneWeb satellite constellation
    • Russian space agency Roscosmos says it will not launch OneWeb's 36 satellites from the Baikonur Cosmodrome, scheduled for Friday, unless OneWeb provided "legally binding guarantees" that they will not be used for military purposes by the United Kingdom. (BBC News)

Sports

  • 2022 Winter Paralympics
    • It is announced that Russia and Belarus will compete in the upcoming Winter Paralympics in Beijing despite their invasion of Ukraine. However, they will be classified as "neutral countries". (The Independent)
