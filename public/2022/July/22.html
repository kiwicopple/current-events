Armed conflicts and attacks

  • Iran–Israel conflict during the Syrian civil war
    • July 2022 Israeli airstrikes near Damascus
      • An Israeli airstrike kills three Syrian soldiers, injures seven others and destroys an Iranian weapons depot near Damascus. (AFP via France 24)
  • Aftermath of the Second Libyan Civil War
    • 13 people are killed as fighting occurs in the Libyan capital of Tripoli. (Reuters)
  • Mali War
    • The Macina Liberation Front launches an attack on Mali's largest military base in Bamako. One soldier and six of the attackers are killed. (Reuters)

Disasters and accidents

  • The main border crossing between Argentina and Chile reopens after several days of closure due to heavy snowfall. (Yahoo! News)

Health and environment

  • COVID-19 pandemic
    • COVID-19 pandemic in Taiwan
      • Taiwan reports their first case of the Omicron BA.2.75 Centaurus variant in a Taiwanese individual arriving from India. (Taiwan News)
  • 2022 monkeypox outbreak]
    • Monaco and Bermuda report their first cases of monkeypox. (News Monaco) (Royal Gazette)
  • 2022 monkeypox outbreak in the United States
    • The first children cases of monkeypox are reported in the United States, with one being in California. (CNN)
    • Maine reports it's first case of monkeypox. (WABI TV)
  • The Nuclear Regulation Authority of Japan formally approves the release of million of tons of treated water from the Fukushima Daiichi Nuclear Power Plant into the Pacific Ocean. The plan had already been endorsed by the Japanese government and other agencies. (Radio France International)

International relations

  • United Kingdom–European Union relations
    • Brexit
      • The European Commission launches four new legal procedures against the United Kingdom for alleged infringements of the Brexit Withdrawal Agreement relating to the passage of the Northern Ireland Protocol Bill. (Al Jazeera)
  • Russia–Ukraine relations
    • 2022 Russian invasion of Ukraine
      • Russia and Ukraine sign a UN-backed agreement to allow the export of millions of tonnes of grain from blockaded Black Sea ports. (The Guardian)
  • North Korea–South Korea relations
    • South Korea announces that it will lift a decades-long ban on public access to North Korean television, newspapers and other media. (AP)
  • Russia–Transnistria relations
    • Transnistrian Foreign Minister Vitaly Ignatyev reaffirms that Transnistria is committed to becoming an independent state and to possibly unite with Russia. (AP)
  • Paraguay–United States relations
    • U.S. Secretary of State Antony Blinken accuses former Paraguayan President Horacio Cartes of "significant corruption". (Yahoo! News)

Law and crime

  • 2022 Sri Lankan protests
    • The military and police raid an anti-government protest camp in Colombo, arresting nine people. (The Sydney Morning Herald)
  • Rohingya genocide case
    • The International Court of Justice rejects the Myanmar military junta’s attempt to challenge a case accusing the country for their role in the Rohingya genocide. (AP)
  • The Israel Police announces that a couple has been placed under house arrest after a man attending a party at the couple's villa in Karmei Yosef died after falling into a sinkhole in the swimming pool. (NBC News)
