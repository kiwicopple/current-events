Armed conflicts and attacks

  • Russo-Ukrainian War
    • 2022 Russian invasion of Ukraine
      • Serhiy Haidai, Governor of Luhansk Oblast, says that Russian artillery continues in places across the oblast and that the 8,000 residents of Sievierodonetsk are on the verge of a "humanitarian catastrophe". (ABC News)
  • Assassination of Shinzo Abe
    • Former Prime Minister of Japan Shinzo Abe is shot in the back while giving a speech in Nara, Japan, and later dies from his injuries. The alleged shooter, Tetsuya Yamagami, a 41-year-old former member of the navy, is arrested. (BBC News)

Business and economy

  • Proposed acquisition of Twitter by Elon Musk
    • The legal team representing entrepreneur Elon Musk files a claim to the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission to officially terminate Musk's deal to purchase Twitter, saying that the company made "false and misleading" statements during negotiations. (The Verge)
  • The Central Bank of Peru increases its interest rate to 6% in an attempt to slow inflation, which reached a 25-year record yesterday. (Infobae)
  • Rogers Communications reports massive outages across Canada, impacting its wireless, cable and internet services, and causing disruptions to banking, government, and emergency services. (CBC News)

Disasters and accidents

  • 2022 European heat wave
    • British authorities issue a level 2 warning as a heat wave continues. (The Independent via MSN)
    • Portugal issues warnings of temperatures as high as 43 °C (109 °F) amid a drought and wildfires in parts of the country. (The Washington Post)

Health and environment

  • 2022 monkeypox outbreak
    • 2022 monkeypox outbreak in the United States
      • South Carolina and West Virginia report their first cases of monkeypox. (WLTX-TV) (WVNS-TV)

International relations

  • Costa Rican President Rodrigo Chaves Robles says that his country will apply to join the Pacific Alliance. (Today)

Law and crime

  • A court in Switzerland acquits Sepp Blatter and Michel Platini of illegally arranging payments while they served FIFA and UEFA. (CNN)
  • Killnet, a Russian hacker group, launches the largest cyberattack in the history of Latvia, targeting the country's public broadcaster. The broadcaster says that the attack was repelled. (The Times)
